Overview of Requirements

Throughout Term 1, you will develop essential engineering skills through hands-on projects and investigations. Here's what you'll accomplish:

  • Safety First: Complete WHS onboarding (OnGuard safety tests) and learn to apply the hierarchy of control in all workshop tasks. Safety is your foundation for everything else.
  • Materials Investigation: Explore material properties (metals, polymers, concrete) and corrosion protection through practical experiments. Discover why engineers choose specific materials for different applications.
  • Water Tower Project: Design, build and test a balsa/pasta Water Tower. Record loads, deflection and failure modes with photos/video. This project teaches you about structural design and testing methods.
  • Concrete Beam Challenge: Cast and load-test a concrete beam. Compare strength-to-weight ratios and discuss reinforcement options. Learn how different materials work together in composite structures.
  • Project Documentation: Maintain a comprehensive project report covering terminology, sketches/drawings, risk management, materials selection, and societal and environmental considerations. This folio demonstrates your engineering thinking.

Outcomes Alignment (IND5)

This term's work addresses multiple Stage 5 Engineering Technology outcomes. Understanding these helps you see how your learning connects to the broader engineering discipline:

  • IND5-1, IND5-3, IND5-4: Workplace Health & Safety outcomes - You'll demonstrate competency through OnGuard tests, SWMS development, proper tool setup, and safe construction of your tower, beam and tensegrity holder projects.
  • IND5-2, IND5-5, IND5-7: Design and production outcomes - Your work will show design iterations, technical sketches/orthogonal drawings, bill of materials, sequencing, and comprehensive testing evidence.
  • IND5-6: Collaboration outcomes - You'll work in teams on tower builds and testing, developing collaborative risk controls and sign-off procedures.
  • IND5-8, IND5-10: Evaluation outcomes - You'll critically evaluate strength-to-weight ratios, corrosion protection methods, and environmental/ethical considerations for materials and structures.
  • IND5-9: Emerging technologies - You'll explore emerging technologies in materials and testing, including using online bridge simulators like PBS Building Big.

Assessment & Folio Checklist

Your assessment folio should be a comprehensive record of your learning journey. Use this checklist to ensure you've included all required evidence:

  • Safety Documentation: Completed SWMS (Safe Work Method Statements) covering hand/power tools used in tower, beam, and tensegrity holder projects. These demonstrate your risk management skills.
  • Project Report: Detailed project report with sketches/orthogonal views, load test data, materials selection rationale, and safety evidence (photos/video). This is your main assessment piece.
  • Experiments Log: Materials experiments log documenting saltwater corrosion tests and damp-proof course brick investigations, including observations and evidence-based conclusions.
  • Critical Reflection: Societal/environmental reflection covering sustainability of materials, corrosion impacts, and safety/legal/ethical considerations. Show you understand the broader implications of engineering decisions.
  • Vocabulary Development: Updated glossary including WHS signage, forces terminology, joint types, and corrosion terms. Engineering communication requires precise language.
  • Certification Evidence: Proof of OnGuard/WHS test completion and any scaffolded responses used. Keep certificates and screenshots as evidence.

Tip: Start your folio early and update it regularly. Don't leave documentation until the end - it's much easier to record observations as you work!

Hands-on & Theory (Term 1 Program)

  • Water tower challenge: design, build, test to failure; capture load data and video.
  • Concrete beam casting and destructive test; compare to tower strength-to-weight.
  • Materials experiments: saltwater soak (mild steel/gal/plastic/concrete) and damp-proof course brick test.
  • Tensegrity phone holder (intro build) to reinforce tension/compression concepts.
  • Bridge concepts: forces, SI units, truss geometry, PBS "Building Big" basics and online challenge.

Week Plan Snapshot (Guide)

This timeline provides a guide for what to expect each week. Remember, learning is flexible - your teacher may adjust timing based on class needs:

  • Week 1: Introduction to engineering fields and career pathways; understanding forces on structures; beginning to think about component massing and structural design.
  • Week 2: Complete WHS/OnGuard safety tests; explore different force types; start designing your water tower; review testing videos to understand failure modes.
  • Week 3: Bridge basics and online challenge tasks; continue building your tower with focus on joint strength and stability.
  • Week 4: Tower report writing begins; construction continues with refinement based on initial testing.
  • Week 5: Explore famous bridges around the world; conduct tower load testing and document results.
  • Weeks 6-8: Cast and test concrete beams; conduct materials experiments (corrosion, DPC); build tensegrity holder and reflect on force distribution.
  • Week 9: Folio completion and catch-up time; compile load-test evidence/photos; finalise corrosion and DPC experiment write-ups.
  • Week 10: Reflection activities and quiz on forces/materials; polish CAD drawings; submit final report and SWMS documentation.
