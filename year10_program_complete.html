PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Course Structure

Year 10 Engineering is organised as two 20-week semester units. Semester 1 develops control systems through the Garden Watering System project. Semester 2 develops alternative energy, mechanical transfer and technical graphics through the Wind Powered USB Phone Charger project.

  • Semester 1: Garden Watering System - control systems, safe low-voltage testing, sensors, actuators, irrigation layout, prototype construction and evaluation.
  • Semester 2: Wind Powered USB Phone Charger - alternative energy, wind generation, impellor design, technical drawing, construction, output testing and sustainability evaluation.

SEMESTER 1: GARDEN WATERING SYSTEM

Duration: 20 weeks

Assessment: Prototype and folio - timing to be confirmed by teacher

Semester 1 Overview

Semester 1 uses a garden watering problem to teach control systems in a practical, workshop-based way. Students move from WHS and input-process-output theory into design, wiring/circuit planning, irrigation layout, prototyping, testing, fault-finding and evaluation.

SEMESTER 1 - WEEK-BY-WEEK SCOPE AND SEQUENCE

Week 1: Unit Introduction, WHS and the Watering Problem

Website Link: Toolkit 1: WHS & Control Systems Safety

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Introduce the project brief: design and build a safe garden watering control-system prototype.
  • Revise workshop expectations, PPE, teacher check points and safe behaviour around water and electricity.
  • Identify the user need: watering plants efficiently, reliably and with controlled water use.
Practical / Activities:
  • Set up folio sections for brief, research, designs, build log, testing and evaluation.
  • Create an initial risk list for water, low-voltage electronics, tools, spills and moving parts.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Folio setup, project brief notes and initial WHS evidence.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-1, IND5-5, IND5-6, IND5-10

Week 2: Inputs, Process, Outputs and Feedback

Website Link: Toolkit 2: Control Systems Concepts

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Define input, process, output and feedback using familiar automated systems.
  • Connect the vocabulary to moisture sensors, rain sensors, switches, pumps, valves and indicators.
  • Discuss open-loop and closed-loop control in student-friendly terms.
Practical / Activities:
  • Draw an input-process-output diagram for a basic watering system.
  • Sort components into input, process and output categories.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Annotated IPO diagram and vocabulary notes.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-2, IND5-5, IND5-7, IND5-9

Week 3: Safe Test Setup

Website Link: Toolkit 1: WHS & Control Systems Safety

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Separate wet zones, dry electronics zones and tool zones.
  • Apply safe low-voltage testing rules and teacher approval before powering circuits.
  • Use risk controls: eliminate, isolate, engineer, administer and PPE.
Practical / Activities:
  • Create a bench layout showing where water, electronics and tools will sit during testing.
  • Complete a risk table for the prototype test environment.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Safe test setup sketch and risk-control table.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-1, IND5-3, IND5-6

Week 4: Irrigation Need, Success Criteria and Folio Direction

Website Links: Semester 1 Overview, Info Sheets

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Describe what makes a watering system successful: reliability, controlled flow, safe operation and sensible water use.
  • Turn the brief into measurable success criteria.
  • Identify what evidence the folio needs to prove design thinking and testing.
Practical / Activities:
  • Write design criteria and constraints for the project.
  • Plan a simple evidence checklist for photos, diagrams, test tables and evaluation notes.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Success criteria, constraints and folio evidence checklist.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-2, IND5-5, IND5-8

Week 5: Sensors and Actuators

Website Link: Toolkit 2: Control Systems Concepts

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Compare sensor and switch options: moisture sensor, rain sensor, float switch, push button and manual override.
  • Compare output options: pump, valve, LED indicator, buzzer or relay-controlled device.
  • Explain why each component must match voltage, current and safe testing limits.
Practical / Activities:
  • Inspect sample components and record function, wiring needs, risks and likely problems.
  • Draft a component selection table.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Component research and selection notes.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-3, IND5-4, IND5-5, IND5-9

Week 6: Control-System Diagrams

Website Link: Toolkit 2: Control Systems Concepts

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Use block diagrams to show how information and energy move through the system.
  • Connect feedback to threshold decisions and repeated testing.
  • Use clear labels so another person can understand the design.
Practical / Activities:
  • Create a neat block diagram for the planned watering system.
  • Annotate each block with its job and evidence needed in the folio.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Block diagram and labelled system explanation.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-2, IND5-5, IND5-7

Week 7: Algorithms and Control Logic

Website Links: Toolkit 2, Design & Test Tutorial

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Use flowcharts or pseudocode to explain watering decisions.
  • Include thresholds, yes/no decisions, timed watering and stop conditions.
  • Discuss how logic improves reliability and reduces wasted water.
Practical / Activities:
  • Draft a watering algorithm for the chosen design.
  • Peer-check whether the algorithm has a clear start, decision and stop.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Flowchart or pseudocode with peer feedback.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-2, IND5-5, IND5-8, IND5-9

Week 8: Component Research and Safe Testing Sequence

Website Link: Toolkit 3: Design & Planning

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Plan subsystem testing before full-system testing.
  • Identify likely faults: leaks, poor pump flow, loose wiring, unreliable triggering and blocked tubing.
  • Prepare teacher check points before water and power are combined.
Practical / Activities:
  • Write a safe testing sequence for water path, input circuit, output device and full integration.
  • Update the risk table based on selected components.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Safe testing sequence and updated risk table.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-1, IND5-3, IND5-5

Week 9: Concept Sketches

Website Link: Toolkit 3: Design & Planning

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Use sketches to explore reservoir position, tubing route, plant area, sensor position and electronics protection.
  • Compare concepts against success criteria, safety and build practicality.
Practical / Activities:
  • Sketch two or three possible system layouts.
  • Annotate strengths, limitations, risks and likely improvements.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Annotated concept sketches.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-2, IND5-4, IND5-5, IND5-8

Week 10: Final Design, Wiring Plan and Irrigation Layout

Website Link: Toolkit 3: Design & Planning

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Turn the chosen concept into a final labelled design.
  • Show the relationship between physical layout, wiring/circuit plan and watering path.
  • Justify the final design against safety, function, reliability and water efficiency.
Practical / Activities:
  • Complete a final design drawing with labels, dimensions and component positions.
  • Create or refine the wiring/circuit plan.
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Final design drawing, wiring plan and design justification.
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-2, IND5-4, IND5-5, IND5-7

Week 11: Materials, Build Sequence and Approval

Website Link: Toolkit 3: Design & Planning

Key Theory / Learning:

Practical / Activities:

Assessment & Evidence:

Practical / Activities:

Assessment & Evidence:

Week 14: Build and Check the Input/Control Circuit

SEMESTER 2: WIND POWERED USB PHONE CHARGER

Duration: 20 weeks

Assessment: Summative - Alternate Energy Project, 40%

Website: year10_term2.html

Semester 2 Overview

Semester 2 uses a compact wind generator to connect alternative energy theory with a practical engineered product. Students investigate renewable energy, design a wind-powered USB charger within constraints, produce final drawings or Onshape evidence, build and test the prototype, and evaluate performance, sustainability and design quality.

IND5-1: Identifies, assesses, applies and manages WHS risks
  • Semester 1: Water/electronics separation, low-voltage testing, spill control, tool safety and teacher check points.
  • Semester 2: Cutting/drilling safety, rotating part controls, generator/USB output testing and supervised mechanical testing.

Website Links: Semester 1 Toolkit 1, Semester 2 Toolkit 4

IND5-2: Applies design principles in project development
  • Semester 1: User need, success criteria, irrigation layout, control logic and prototype refinement.
  • Semester 2: Renewable energy context, impellor options, frame design, drive choice and final design justification.

  • Semester 1: Tubing, reservoir, structure/base material, pump/valve option, wiring and sensor selection.

  • Semester 1: IPO diagrams, wiring plans, flowcharts, build log, test tables and evaluation.

  • Semester 1: Reliability, water efficiency, smart irrigation, automation, agriculture and sustainability.

PROGRAM NOTES

Teaching Approach

  • Project-first learning: Each semester uses a practical product to anchor theory, safety, design, production and evaluation.
  • Evidence-rich folios: Students should collect diagrams, sketches, photos, test tables, fault logs and evaluation notes as the project develops.
  • Test before polish: The strongest evidence comes from testing, faults, modifications and retesting, not just a neat final product.
  • Teacher check points: Use explicit approval points before powered testing, water/electronics integration and rotating output testing.

Differentiation Strategies

  • Provide labelled diagrams, vocabulary lists, worked examples and partially completed test tables for students who need structure.
  • Use simplified approved circuits or mechanical layouts where safety and success need protecting.
  • Offer extension through controlled variable testing, improved CAD/technical drawing, refined mechanisms or deeper sustainability analysis.
  • Allow evidence through photos, diagrams, short written responses and teacher conference notes where appropriate.

Safety Considerations

  • Semester 1: Keep water and electronics separated until approved; use low-voltage systems only; control spills; test subsystems before integration.
  • Semester 2: Guard rotating parts where needed; supervise output testing; manage cutting/drilling risks; check blade stability and frame support.
  • Both semesters: Maintain PPE, tool rules, tidy workspaces, teacher check points and documented risk controls.

Resource Requirements

  • Semester 1: Low-voltage electronics, sensors/switches, pumps or valves, tubing, reservoirs, base materials, hand tools and test equipment.
  • Semester 2: Generator/USB units, blade materials, shafts, bearings/supports, gears/cogs where used, frame materials, hand tools and low-voltage test equipment.
  • Digital: Website toolkits, guided portfolios, info sheets, Google Classroom topics, Onshape where appropriate and teacher-provided Drive resources.

PROGRAM ALIGNMENT SUMMARY

  • Course Progression: Semester 1 builds control-system thinking; Semester 2 extends design, mechanical transfer, energy systems and technical graphics.
  • Assessment Alignment: Both semester projects require research, design development, safe construction, testing evidence and evaluation.
  • Website Alignment: Each unit links to a semester hub, toolkit pages, assessment/support documents, portfolio resources and printable info sheets.
  • Student Readiness: The sequence deliberately moves from brief and theory to design, build, test, improve and evaluate.
