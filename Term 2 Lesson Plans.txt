================================================================================
WEEK 1: Introduction to Tensegrity & Forces in Equilibrium
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Understand the concept of tensegrity structures
- Identify tension and compression forces in equilibrium
- Begin material selection thinking for tension vs compression applications
- Review workshop safety for Term 2 projects

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Show images/video of tensegrity structures (sculptures, bridges, architectural applications)
- Discussion: "What do you notice about these structures? How do they stay up?"
- Introduce key terms: tensegrity, tension, compression, equilibrium, preload

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

Theory Session (20 min):
- PPT: Forces in equilibrium – how tension and compression work together
- Material properties: why cables work in tension, struts in compression
- Examples: suspension bridges (cables in tension), columns (compression)
- Worksheet: Identify tension/compression in everyday structures

Practical Session (30 min):
- Mini tensegrity model build: simple 3-strut model using dowels and string
- Students work in pairs to construct basic tensegrity structure
- Observe how preload creates stability
- Document process with photos

Workshop Safety Review (10 min):
- Walk-through of hazards for Term 2: cutting struts, drilling holes, tensioning cables
- Review PPE requirements: safety glasses, dust masks, gloves
- Discuss heat sources if soldering will be used

PLENARY (10 min):
- Share observations from mini model builds
- Key takeaway: "Tensegrity structures balance tension and compression"
- Preview Week 2: Full tensegrity prism build

ASSESSMENT:
- Formative: Participation in discussion, completion of tension/compression worksheet
- Start vocab dictionary: tensegrity, preload, tension, compression, equilibrium

RESOURCES:
- PPT on tensegrity structures
- Mini model materials: dowels, string, drill
- Tension/compression worksheet
- Vocab dictionary template

================================================================================
WEEK 2: Tensegrity Prism Build
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Build a complete tensegrity prism structure
- Apply safe cutting and drilling techniques
- Understand preload sequence and its importance
- Document build process with photos and notes

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Review tensegrity geometry: strut placement, cable routing
- Discuss material choices: what makes a good strut? What makes a good cable?
- Show example of completed tensegrity prism

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

Workshop Session (50 min):
- Cut struts to length: use saw, measure accurately, file smooth edges
- Drill holes for cable attachment: mark positions, drill carefully, deburr holes
- Attach cables: route cables correctly, tie secure knots or use hardware
- Apply preload: tension cables systematically, check for stability
- Test structure: apply small loads, measure deflection, observe behaviour

Documentation (10 min):
- Take photos of construction steps
- Record preload sequence: which cables were tensioned first, how much tension
- Note any challenges or modifications made during build

PLENARY (10 min):
- Compare structures: which designs are most stable? Why?
- Discuss preload importance: "What happens if cables aren't tensioned enough?"
- Preview Week 3: Renewable energy investigations

ASSESSMENT:
- Evidence: Tensegrity build photos, preload sequence notes, load/deflection measurements
- Draft SWMS for cutting/drilling struts and tensioning cables

RESOURCES:
- Workshop tools: saws, drills, files, measuring tools
- Materials: dowels/rod for struts, cord/wire for cables
- Scales for load testing
- Camera/phone for photos

WEEK 3: Alternate Energies – Wind Energy
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Understand energy transformations (mechanical to electrical)
- Explore wind energy principles and blade design
- Conduct wind turbine experiments with data collection
- Calculate power output and efficiency

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Video: Wind farms and how they work
- Discussion: "Where have you seen wind turbines? Why are they important?"
- Introduce energy transformation: kinetic energy → electrical energy

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

Theory Session (15 min):
- PPT: Wind energy basics – how turbines convert wind to electricity
- Blade design principles: angle of attack, number of blades, blade shape
- Electrical concepts: voltage, current, power (P = VI)
- Efficiency: what affects how much energy we can capture?

Practical Investigation (35 min):
- Set up wind turbine: DC motor as generator, fan as wind source, multimeter
- Test different blade designs: 
  * Design 1: 3 blades, flat shape
  * Design 2: 3 blades, curved shape
  * Design 3: 6 blades, flat shape
- Record data: voltage (V), current (A) for each design
- Calculate power: P = V × I for each design
- Compare results: which design produces most power? Why?

Data Analysis (10 min):
- Create data table: blade design, voltage, current, power
- Discuss results: what factors affected power output?
- Efficiency reflection: how could we improve the design?

PLENARY (10 min):
- Share results: which blade design was most effective?
- Key takeaway: "Blade design affects energy capture efficiency"
- Preview Week 4: Solar energy investigation

ASSESSMENT:
- Evidence: Wind turbine test data table, power calculations, energy investigation log entry

RESOURCES:
- DC motors (as generators), fans, multimeters
- Materials for blades: card, balsa, plastic
- Data recording sheets
- Calculators

WEEK 4: Solar Energy Investigation
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Understand solar photovoltaic principles
- Investigate factors affecting solar panel performance
- Evaluate sustainability of solar energy
- Conduct solar panel experiments with systematic data collection

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Video: Solar farms and rooftop solar installations
- Discussion: "How does sunlight become electricity?"
- Introduce PV (photovoltaic) cells

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

Theory Session (15 min):
- PPT: Solar photovoltaic principles – how solar cells work
- Factors affecting performance: angle to sun, shading, temperature, light intensity
- Solar applications: panels, solar ovens, concentrated solar power
- Sustainability: embodied energy, lifecycle analysis, recycling

Practical Investigation (35 min):
- Set up solar panel experiment: small PV cells, multimeter, variable light source (lamp)
- Test 1: Angle to light source
  * Measure voltage/current at 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90° angles
  * Record data for each angle
- Test 2: Distance from light source
  * Measure voltage/current at different distances (10cm, 20cm, 30cm, 40cm)
  * Record data for each distance
- Test 3: Light intensity (if possible)
  * Vary lamp brightness, measure output
- Calculate power for each test condition

Optional: Solar Oven Build (if time):
- Build simple solar oven: reflectors, insulation, temperature measurement
- Test temperature achieved, compare to ambient temperature

Data Analysis (10 min):
- Create data tables: angle vs output, distance vs output
- Graph results: identify optimal angle and distance
- Discuss: "What conditions give best solar panel performance?"

Sustainability Reflection (10 min):
- Discuss embodied energy: energy used to manufacture solar panels
- Lifecycle analysis: manufacturing, use, end-of-life
- Recycling considerations: what happens to old solar panels?
- Reflection: "Is solar energy truly sustainable?"

PLENARY (10 min):
- Share findings: optimal angle and distance for solar panels
- Key takeaway: "Solar panel performance depends on positioning and conditions"
- Preview Week 5: Hydro/wave energy

ASSESSMENT:
- Evidence: Solar panel test data tables, efficiency calculations, sustainability reflection notes
- Folio updated: solar energy section added to energy investigation log

RESOURCES:
- Small PV cells, multimeters, lamps/variable light sources
- Protractors for angle measurement, rulers for distance
- Data recording sheets
- Optional: Materials for solar oven (cardboard, foil, insulation)

WEEK 5: Hydro/Wave Energy & Energy Comparison
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Understand hydroelectric and wave energy principles
- Build and test a simple hydro device
- Compare renewable energy sources (wind, solar, hydro)
- Create energy transformation diagrams

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Video: Hydroelectric dams and wave energy devices
- Discussion: "How does moving water create electricity?"
- Introduce potential energy → kinetic energy → electrical energy

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

Theory Session (15 min):
- PPT: Hydroelectric principles – dams vs run-of-river systems
- Wave energy concepts: how wave motion drives generators
- Energy transformations: potential → kinetic → electrical
- Power calculations review: P = VI, efficiency calculations

Practical Investigation (35 min):
- Build simple hydro device: water wheel or wave car
  * Option 1: Water wheel with DC motor/generator
  * Option 2: Wave car (floating device that moves with waves)
- Test hydro device:
  * Vary water flow rate (for water wheel)
  * Test in wave tank or container (for wave car)
  * Measure voltage and current output
- Record data: flow rate vs voltage/current, calculate power
- Compare to wind and solar: create comparison table

Energy Comparison Activity (15 min):
- Create comparison table: Wind vs Solar vs Hydro
  * Energy source, how it works, advantages, disadvantages, efficiency, real-world applications
- Energy transformation diagrams: sketch energy flow for each source
- Discussion: "Which renewable energy source is best? Why?"

PLENARY (10 min):
- Share comparison tables and transformation diagrams
- Key takeaway: "Different renewable energy sources suit different locations and applications"
- Complete energy investigation log: all three sources documented

ASSESSMENT:
- Evidence: Hydro device test data, energy comparison table, energy transformation diagrams
- Complete energy investigation log with all three energy sources

RESOURCES:
- Materials for hydro device: DC motors, water wheels, containers, water
- Multimeters, data recording sheets
- Comparison table template
- Energy transformation diagram templates

WEEK 6: Buoyancy Theory & Submarine Introduction
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Understand Archimedes' principle and buoyancy
- Calculate density and buoyancy forces
- Research submarine design and ballast systems
- Begin submarine design sketches

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Video: Submarines diving and surfacing
- Discussion: "How do submarines control their depth?"
- Introduce key terms: buoyancy, ballast, density, displacement

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

Theory Session (25 min):
- PPT: Buoyancy principles – Archimedes' principle
- Density calculations: ρ = m/V
- Buoyancy force: F_b = ρ_fluid × V_displaced × g
- Neutral buoyancy: when weight equals buoyancy force
- Pressure and depth: how water pressure increases with depth
- Submarine ballast control: how ballast tanks work

Worksheet Activity (20 min):
- Density calculations: calculate density for various materials (water, air, wood, metal, etc.)
- Buoyancy force calculations: practice problems
- Compare densities: which materials float? Which sink? Why?

Research Activity (15 min):
- "Submarines Around the World" research task
- Identify key features: ballast systems, depth capabilities, uses
- Research: How do different submarines control depth?
- Record findings in notes

Design Activity (10 min):
- Begin submarine design: sketch bottle submarine
- Include: ballast tank, air volume control mechanism, weight distribution
- Label key features: ballast system, air volume control, trim weights

PLENARY (10 min):
- Share density calculations: discuss which materials float/sink
- Key takeaway: "Density determines whether objects float or sink"
- Preview Week 7: Submarine build and ballast experiments

ASSESSMENT:
- Evidence: Buoyancy calculations worksheet, submarine research notes, initial submarine design sketch

RESOURCES:
- PPT on buoyancy and submarines
- Density calculations worksheet
- Research resources: internet access, books
- Design paper, pencils, rulers

================================================================================
WEEK 7: Submarine Build & Ballast Experiments
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Build a functional bottle submarine with ballast control
- Conduct systematic ballast experiments
- Measure density and calculate buoyancy
- Test stability and trim

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Review submarine design sketches
- Discuss ballast control mechanisms: syringes, air bladders, weights
- Show example of completed submarine

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

Workshop Session (40 min):
- Build bottle submarine:
  * Prepare bottle: clean, mark waterline
  * Attach ballast system: syringe or air bladder for air volume control
  * Add weights: attach weights for trim and stability
  * Test basic function: does it float? Can you control depth?
- Ballast experiments:
  * Test 1: Air volume vs depth
    - Record air volume (ml) and corresponding depth (cm)
    - Create data table: air volume, depth, observations
  * Test 2: Dive/surface cycles
    - Test multiple dive/surface cycles
    - Observe: does it return to same depth? Is it consistent?
  * Test 3: Neutral buoyancy
    - Find the air volume where submarine neither sinks nor rises
    - Record this neutral buoyancy point

Density Experiments (15 min):
- Measure mass and volume of submarine components
- Calculate density: ρ = m/V for each component
- Calculate overall submarine density
- Compare to water density: why does it float/sink?

Stability Tests (10 min):
- Observe submarine behaviour: pitch (nose up/down), roll (side to side), yaw (rotation)
- Adjust weight distribution: move weights to improve stability
- Test trim: does submarine sit level? Adjust if needed

PLENARY (10 min):
- Share results: air volume vs depth data, neutral buoyancy points
- Discuss stability: what weight distribution worked best?
- Key takeaway: "Ballast control requires understanding density and buoyancy"
- Preview Week 8: CAD introduction

ASSESSMENT:
- Evidence: Submarine build photos, ballast test data (air volume vs depth), density calculations, stability notes
- SWMS for water-based experiments completed

RESOURCES:
- Materials: plastic bottles, syringes/air bladders, weights, tubing
- Water containers/tanks for testing
- Scales, rulers, measuring cylinders
- Camera/phone for photos

WEEK 8: CAD Introduction & Onshape Basics
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Understand CAD fundamentals and parametric modelling
- Navigate Onshape interface
- Create basic sketches and features
- Model tensegrity components (struts, caps)

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Video: CAD in engineering and manufacturing
- Discussion: "Why do engineers use CAD? What are the advantages?"
- Introduce Onshape: cloud-based CAD software

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

ICT Session – Onshape Setup (10 min):
- Create Onshape accounts (if not already done)
- Navigate interface: documents, parts, assemblies, drawings
- Explore workspace: sketch tools, feature tools, view controls

Tutorial Session (30 min):
- Complete Onshape tutorials:
  * Tutorial 1: Basic sketching (lines, circles, rectangles, dimensions)
  * Tutorial 2: Extruding sketches to create 3D parts
  * Tutorial 3: Adding features (holes, fillets, chamfers)
- Practice: Follow step-by-step tutorial to create simple part

Practical Modelling (20 min):
- Model tensegrity components:
  * Part 1: Tensegrity strut
    - Sketch: circular profile, dimension diameter and length
    - Extrude: create cylindrical strut
    - Add holes: drill holes for cable attachment (if modelling detail)
  * Part 2: Tensegrity cap/end piece
    - Sketch: profile for cap
    - Extrude: create cap shape
    - Add features: holes, fillets as needed
- Document process: take screenshots of sketches, features, final parts

PLENARY (10 min):
- Share CAD models: show struts and caps created
- Discuss challenges: "What was difficult? What was easy?"
- Key takeaway: "CAD allows us to model designs before building"
- Preview Week 9: CAD assembly and technical drawings

ASSESSMENT:
- Evidence: Onshape account setup, tutorial completion, CAD model screenshots (struts, caps)
- CAD log started in folio

RESOURCES:
- Computer access with internet
- Onshape accounts (free educational accounts)
- Onshape tutorials (built-in or provided)
- Screenshot tools

WEEK 9: CAD Assembly & Technical Drawings
================================================================================

LESSON OBJECTIVES:
- Create CAD assemblies using mates and constraints
- Generate technical drawings with orthographic views
- Add dimensions and annotations to drawings
- Compare CAD models to physical builds

STARTER ACTIVITY (10 min):
- Review CAD models from Week 8
- Discuss assemblies: "How do we put parts together in CAD?"
- Introduce technical drawings: "How do engineers communicate designs?"

MAIN ACTIVITIES:

Assembly Session (25 min):
- Create new assembly in Onshape
- Insert parts: add struts and caps to assembly
- Add mates: constrain parts together
  * Coincident mates: align parts
  * Parallel mates: keep parts parallel
  * Distance mates: set distances between parts
- Create simplified tensegrity assembly: represent struts and cable connections
- Test assembly: move parts to check constraints work correctly

Technical Drawing Session (25 min):
- Generate technical drawings:
  * Create new drawing from part
  * Add orthographic views: front, top, side views
  * Add isometric view: 3D view for clarity
  * Add dimensions: length, diameter, hole positions
  * Add annotations: notes, title block
- Create drawings for key components: strut, cap
- Optional: Create drawing for renewable energy component (wind turbine blade, solar panel mount)

Comparison Activity (10 min):
- Measure actual parts: use calipers/rulers to measure physical tensegrity components
- Compare to CAD: check CAD dimensions match actual dimensions
- Discuss differences: "Why might there be differences? How accurate is CAD?"

PLENARY (10 min):
- Share technical drawings: show orthographic views with dimensions
- Discuss: "How do technical drawings help communicate designs?"
- Key takeaway: "CAD and technical drawings are essential engineering communication tools"
- Complete CAD section in folio

ASSESSMENT:
- Evidence: CAD assembly screenshots, technical drawings (orthographic views with dimensions), comparison notes
- CAD section completed in folio

RESOURCES:
- Onshape access
- CAD models from Week 8
- Measuring tools: calipers, rulers
- Technical drawing standards reference
