Outcomes to be assessed

  • IND5-1: Identifies, assesses, applies, and manages the risks and WHS issues associated with the use of tools, equipment, materials, processes, and technologies (theory focus).
  • IND5-5: Selects, interprets, and applies suitable communication techniques in the development and presentation of ideas and projects.
  • IND5-6: Identifies and participates in collaborative work practices in the learning environment (as reflected in scenario-based questions).
  • IND5-9: Describes, analyses, and uses a range of current, new, and emerging technologies and their applications.
  • IND5-10: Describes, analyses, and evaluates the impact of technology on society, the environment, and cultural issues locally and globally.

Goals for this activity

In this exam you will be expected to:

  • Demonstrate your knowledge and understanding of the engineering concepts covered in Terms 1 and 2.
  • Apply WHS knowledge to workshop and theoretical scenarios.
  • Interpret and produce technical diagrams and sketches.
  • Analyse engineering problems and communicate solutions clearly.
  • Evaluate the impact of technologies on society and the environment.

Task Outline

The final examination will assess your understanding of key engineering content from throughout the year, including:

  • WHS, risk management, and workshop safety culture.
  • Materials and corrosion; concrete as a composite; reinforcement and curing.
  • Structures: forces, load paths, struts and ties, triangulation, and tower/beam behaviour.
  • Tensegrity concepts; tension and compression in engineered systems.
  • Alternate energies and basic energy systems.
  • Submarine design and buoyancy (density, stability, ballast).
  • CAD and communication of engineering ideas.
  • Environmental and societal impacts of technologies.

Exam format

  • Multiple-choice questions.
  • Short-answer responses.
  • Technical diagrams and sketching tasks (e.g. forces on structures, simple mechanisms).
  • Structured and extended response questions based on engineering scenarios.

Criteria

You will be assessed on how well you:

  • Recall and apply engineering terminology and concepts.
  • Apply WHS principles to given situations.
  • Interpret and produce clear technical diagrams and graphical information.
  • Structure extended responses logically and support them with relevant engineering ideas.
  • Explain the impact of technologies on society and the environment.

Marking Criteria (summary)
