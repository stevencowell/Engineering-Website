Outcomes to be assessed

This task assesses all Year 9 Industrial Technology – Engineering outcomes:

  • IND5-1 to IND5-10

Goals for this activity

In this task you will be expected to:

  • Maintain safe work practices using more complex tools, equipment, and processes.
  • Produce a high-quality engineering project based on Term 2 concepts (tensegrity, alternate energies, submarine design, and/or CAD-based projects).
  • Use CAD/Onshape and other technologies to communicate and refine your design.
  • Document the complete development of your project in a detailed engineering folio.
  • Evaluate the performance, efficiency, and environmental impact of your solution.

Task Outline

This is the second major engineering project for the course. It is based on Term 2 learning, including: tensegrity structures, alternate energy systems, submarine design and buoyancy, and the development of CAD/Onshape models. You will design, construct, and test an engineered solution and document your work in a comprehensive folio.

Project requirements

  • Develop a clear design brief and set of specifications for your chosen engineering project.
  • Use appropriate design processes (sketching, modelling, CAD) to plan your solution.
  • Construct a functional engineered product that demonstrates:
    • Correct application of engineering concepts (e.g. tension/compression balance, buoyancy, stability, energy transfer).
    • Effective material and process selection.
    • Consistent adherence to WHS requirements.
  • Produce a detailed engineering folio that includes:
    • Design brief, research notes, and concept sketches.
    • CAD/Onshape screenshots, drawings, or models.
    • Planning documentation (sequence of operations, tools, materials).
    • WHS and risk management notes for key processes.
    • Testing methods and results (e.g. effectiveness, efficiency, stability, performance).
    • Evaluation and reflection using appropriate engineering terminology.

Criteria

In this task you will be assessed on how well you:

  • Apply WHS practices and work safely and cooperatively in the workshop.
  • Produce a complex, functional engineering project demonstrating good workmanship and accuracy.
  • Use CAD/Onshape and other communication techniques to clearly present your design.
  • Document your project thoroughly in a well-structured folio.
  • Evaluate your project’s performance, efficiency, and impact, and propose realistic improvements.

Marking Criteria (summary)
