Water Tower Project Instructions

Project brief

Build a tower that safely carries heavy weight using the supplied materials.

  • Height: At least 300 mm tall.
  • Mass: No more than 60 g.
  • Load: Must hold at least 25 kg.
  • Time: Must hold that load for at least 5 seconds.
  • Top support: Must hold a 300 mm square board during testing.

What ‚Äúgood‚Äù looks like: triangles/bracing to stop racking, neat strong joints, a clear load path (top ‚Üí legs/columns ‚Üí base) and a stable base.

Materials & evidence

  • 4 mm square cross-section plywood members.
  • 300 mm square board for the test platform.
  • Cast iron weights placed on top during testing.

Hand in: your built tower, folio/report showing your process, and testing results (mass, load held, hold time).

Build steps

1
Sketch 2‚Äì3 ideas

Choose a design that is strong and light. Use triangles.

More tips
  • Sketch a few tower shapes to avoid locking in too early.
  • Show the load path: top plate ‚Üí legs ‚Üí base.
  • Avoid long, unsupported pieces (they bend and twist).

Quick check: Can you point to at least six triangles?

2
Draw your final design with measurements

Show height and where the top board will sit.

More tips
  • Add key measurements: height, base width, brace lengths.
  • Label important parts: legs, braces, gussets, top support.
  • Mark where the top board sits and how it is supported.

Quick check: Could someone else build it from your drawing?

3
Plan your cuts

Work out what pieces you need before you start cutting.

More tips
  • Write a cut list: piece name, quantity, length (mm).
  • Plan build order: base ‚Üí legs ‚Üí braces ‚Üí top support.
  • Allow a little extra length for trimming and squaring.

Quick check: Do you have bracing to stop sideways wobble?

4
Build the base and frame

Start square and straight. Bad bases make bad towers.

More tips
  • Dry-fit first (no glue) to check alignment.
  • Use a try-square / set square to keep corners true.
  • Glue and clamp ‚Äî don‚Äôt rush the cure time.

Quick check: Is your base flat on the bench with no rocking?

5
Add bracing and gussets

Triangles = strength. Gussets = strong joints.

More tips
  • Add braces where members are long or unsupported.
  • Gussets should cover the joint properly (not tiny stickers).
  • Keep both sides symmetrical where possible.

Quick check: If you gently twist it, does it resist twisting?

6
Test and record

Load it safely, record results, and learn from the failure mode.

More tips
  • Test in small steps (don‚Äôt jump straight to max load).
  • Record: load, deflection, and where it bends or cracks first.
  • Use correct words: compression, tension, bending, buckling.

Quick check: Did you write down results as you went (not later)?

Quick checks

  • Can you point to at least six triangles in your design?
  • Is the base flat on the bench with no rocking?
  • Does the tower resist twisting when you gently check it?
  • Have you captured photos and test data for your folio?

Overview of Requirements

Throughout Term 1, you will develop essential engineering skills through hands-on projects and investigations. Here's what you'll accomplish:

  • Safety First: Complete WHS onboarding (OnGuard safety tests) and learn to apply the hierarchy of control in all workshop tasks. Safety is your foundation for everything else.
  • Structural Concepts (Weeks 2-3): Master forces, SI units, truss geometry, and load paths. These concepts form the foundation for your tower design.
  • Water Tower Design & Planning (Weeks 2-3): Create detailed tower layouts, plan joints, select materials, and develop build sequences. Good planning here saves time and improves build quality.
  • Water Tower Build, Test & Evaluate (Weeks 3-5): Build your tower based on your design, conduct systematic load tests, and document everything in a comprehensive folio. This is your main Term 1 project.
  • Project Documentation: Maintain a comprehensive project folio covering design rationale, technical sketches, build log, test data, evaluation, and reflection. This demonstrates your engineering thinking and understanding of structural concepts.

Outcomes Alignment (IND5)

This term's work addresses multiple Stage 5 Engineering Technology outcomes. Understanding these helps you see how your learning connects to the broader engineering discipline:

  • IND5-1, IND5-3, IND5-4: Workplace Health & Safety outcomes - You'll demonstrate competency through OnGuard tests, SWMS development, proper tool setup, and safe construction of your water tower project.
  • IND5-2, IND5-5, IND5-7: Design and production outcomes - Your work will show design iterations, technical sketches/orthogonal drawings, bill of materials, sequencing, and comprehensive testing evidence.
  • IND5-6: Collaboration outcomes - You'll work in teams on tower builds and testing, developing collaborative risk controls and sign-off procedures.
  • IND5-8, IND5-10: Evaluation outcomes - You'll critically evaluate strength-to-weight ratios, load paths, failure modes, and environmental/ethical considerations for materials and structures.
  • IND5-9: Emerging technologies - You'll explore emerging technologies in structural design and testing, including using online bridge simulators like PBS Building Big.

Assessment & Folio Checklist

Your assessment folio should be a comprehensive record of your learning journey. Use this checklist to ensure you've included all required evidence:

  • Safety Documentation: Completed SWMS (Safe Work Method Statements) covering hand/power tools used in your tower project. These demonstrate your risk management skills.
  • Project Report: Detailed project folio with design sketches/orthogonal views, build log, load test data, materials selection rationale, and safety evidence (photos/video). This is your main assessment piece.
  • Design Documentation: Design brief analysis, preliminary sketches, final design drawings with annotations (load paths, member roles, joint details), cutting list, and build sequence.
  • Critical Reflection: Evaluation covering what worked well, what failed first and why, improvements you would make, and links back to structural concepts from Toolkit 2. Show you understand the broader implications of engineering decisions.
  • Vocabulary Development: Updated glossary including WHS signage, forces terminology, joint types, and structural terms. Engineering communication requires precise language.
  • Certification Evidence: Proof of OnGuard/WHS test completion and any scaffolded responses used. Keep certificates and screenshots as evidence.

Tip: Start your folio early and update it regularly. Don't leave documentation until the end - it's much easier to record observations as you work!

Hands-on & Theory (Term 1 Program)

Project Sequence: Projects are sequenced to allow focused learning and complete folio documentation for each before moving to the next.

  • Weeks 2-3: Structural concepts (Toolkit 2) - forces, SI units, truss geometry, PBS "Building Big" basics and online challenge. Water tower design & planning (Toolkit 3) - design brief analysis, layouts, joint planning, materials selection.
  • Weeks 3-5: Water tower build, test and evaluation (Toolkit 4) - construct tower, conduct load tests, document everything in comprehensive folio.
  • Weeks 6-10: Consolidation, folio completion, reflection, and review of structural concepts.

Week Plan Snapshot (Guide)

This timeline provides a guide for what to expect each week. The schedule is designed to space out major projects, allowing you to complete each one thoroughly before moving to the next. Remember, learning is flexible - your teacher may adjust timing based on class needs:

  • Week 1: Introduction to engineering fields and career pathways. Complete Toolkit 1 (WHS & Risk Control) foundation work - OnGuard tests, PPE, hierarchy of control, SWMS development.
  • Weeks 2-3: Complete Toolkit 2 (Structural Concepts) - forces, SI units, truss geometry, load paths, tension/compression, buckling. Use PBS Bridge Basics/Challenge simulators. Complete Toolkit 3 (Water Tower Design & Planning) - design brief analysis, create tower layouts, plan joints, select materials, develop build sequence, produce technical sketches.
  • Weeks 3-5: Complete Toolkit 4 (Water Tower Build, Test & Evaluate) - build tower based on your design, conduct systematic load tests, record data, document everything in comprehensive folio. Complete your tower folio by end of Week 5.
  • Weeks 6-10: Consolidation and review - polish folio, complete any outstanding documentation, reflect on learning, review structural concepts, submit final reports and SWMS documentation.

Note: The water tower is your main Term 1 project. Complete Toolkits 1-3 before starting your build in Toolkit 4. This sequential approach ensures you have the knowledge and planning in place before construction. Complete your tower folio by the end of Week 5.
