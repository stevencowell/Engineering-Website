Overview of Requirements

Term 4 is your capstone build for the geared lolly dispenser. You will take a design from CAD through manufacture, assembly, testing and showcase.

  • Material readiness: Select timber/sheet goods, create cut lists, and plan safe fabrication with PPE, jigs and clamping.
  • CAD-to-make: Produce assemblies with configs/variables, then generate production drawings and DXF/DWG exports.
  • CAM & manufacture: Prepare kerf/fit allowances, toolpaths and machine setups; cut/print parts and assemble mechanisms.
  • Testing & QA: Run dispense, torque/effort and durability tests; capture data and iterate fixes.
  • Folio & showcase: Finalise drawings, costing, sustainability and safety summaries; present a working dispenser.

Outcomes Alignment (IND5)

  • IND5-1, IND5-3, IND5-4: WHS, SWMS, tool/machine setup, safe CAM/laser/CNC use and guarded mechanisms.
  • IND5-2, IND5-5, IND5-7: Advanced CAD, assemblies, BOMs, costing, production drawings and build sequencing.
  • IND5-6: Collaborative build/test cycles; peer QA and shared risk controls during fabrication.
  • IND5-8, IND5-9, IND5-10: Sustainability of materials and processes, design evaluation, communication and showcase presentation.

Assessment & Folio Checklist

  • SWMS and machine setup checklists (laser/CNC, drill press, sanding) plus PPE evidence.
  • CAD pack: assemblies, drawings, DXF/DWG exports with title blocks and revision notes.
  • Manufacturing evidence: cut files, kerf notes, fit checks, photos of assembly/guards.
  • Testing data: dispense counts, jams, torque/effort, durability observations with photos/video.
  • Folio sections: costing, sustainability, safety, QA sign-offs, and a short showcase script/board.

Hands-on & Theory (Term 4 Program)

  • Timber/material prep: select stock, plan cuts, practise safe sawing/drilling/sanding.
  • CAD refinement: configs/variables, mates, drawings, export-ready DXF/DWG.
  • CAM & fabrication: kerf allowances, toolpaths, safe machine setup, part cutting/printing.
  • Assembly & tuning: gears/cams/ratchet alignment, guards, fasteners/adhesives.
  • Testing & iteration: dispense trials, jam fixes, torque/effort checks, durability runs.
  • Folio & showcase: data tables/graphs, sustainability + safety summaries, presentation assets.

Resources & Differentiation

  • Materials: ply/timber sheets, acrylic guards, shafts/dowels, fasteners, adhesives.
  • Digital: Onshape accounts, laser/CNC software, DXF/DWG export guides.
  • Scaffolds: SWMS templates, kerf/fit checklist, costing spreadsheet, folio structure.
  • Differentiation: exemplar CAD packs, simplified cut files, multilingual captions, extension to multi-flavour/dual-cam mechanisms.

Week Plan Snapshot (Guide)

  • W1: Timber/material selection, cut lists, WHS refresh, jigs/clamping.
  • W2: CAD updates (assemblies/configs); prep drawings and export DXF/DWG.
  • W3: Kerf/fit checks; generate toolpaths; machine setup briefings.
  • W4: Cut/print parts; begin sub-assemblies; start guards.
  • W5: Main assembly of gears/cams/ratchet; alignment and fasteners.
  • W6: Testing cycle 1: dispense counts, jams, torque/effort; log data.
  • W7: Iteration: adjust fits, add guides/guards; re-test and update folio.
  • W8: Durability testing; QA checklist; costing and sustainability notes.
  • W9: Finalise folio, drawings, and showcase board/script; polish device.
  • W10: Showcase/demo, reflection, final submissions (CAD pack, folio, SWMS).
