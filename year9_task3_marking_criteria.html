Year 9 Industrial Technology – Engineering

Task 3 – Final Examination

Marking Criteria

Total: 100 marks (scaled to 30%)

Task 3 Marking Criteria
Criterion High (A-range) Sound (B–C range) Basic (D–E range)
1. Knowledge & Understanding of Engineering Concepts (IND5-5, IND5-9, IND5-10)
40 marks		 Demonstrates comprehensive knowledge; accurate explanations; confidently applies concepts to new and unfamiliar problems. Understands key ideas with some minor errors; can apply concepts in familiar contexts. Limited understanding; confusing or incorrect explanations; difficulty applying concepts.
2. WHS & Risk Management (theory) (IND5-1)
20 marks		 Provides detailed, accurate WHS responses; shows high understanding of risk control, PPE, processes, and workshop systems. Basic WHS knowledge; identifies most hazards correctly; some gaps in control strategies. Incorrect or limited WHS knowledge; cannot identify key risks or controls.
3. Communication & Technical Diagrams (IND5-5)
20 marks		 Diagrams are clear and accurate; uses correct conventions and terminology; labels are precise; demonstrates strong graphical communication. Diagrams mostly correct with minor errors; terminology adequate. Diagrams unclear, incorrect, or missing; poor or incorrect terminology.
4. Extended Response / Application (IND5-6, IND5-9, IND5-10)
20 marks		 Well-structured, logical responses; excellent application of engineering principles; considers function, sustainability, and impacts. Reasonable structure and logic; mostly relevant; limited depth in analysis. Disorganised or minimal responses; little application of engineering knowledge.

These criteria should be used in conjunction with the official Task 3 Assessment Notification.
