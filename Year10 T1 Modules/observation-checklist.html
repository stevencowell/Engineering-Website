Safety Prep

Complete these checks before any powered test. If water reaches electronics, stop the test and get the teacher. No heroic nonsense.

  • Wet zone and dry electronics zone are clearly separated.
  • Teacher has checked the circuit, power source and controller before power is connected.
  • Reservoir, tray, tubing and outlet are stable and cannot tip or pull loose during testing.
  • Spill control is ready: tray, towel, container, sink area or teacher-approved drainage point.
  • Hands are dry before touching switches, batteries, controllers or wiring.

Subsystem Testing

Output Test

  • Pump, valve or output device works by itself.
  • Tubing does not kink, leak or disconnect during a short test.
  • Water delivered is measured in millilitres for a fixed time.
  • Output stops when switched off or when the control signal ends.

Input Test

  • Sensor, switch, timer or trigger changes state when expected.
  • Dry/wet threshold or trigger condition is recorded.
  • Input can be reset and tested again without rebuilding the circuit.
  • False triggers or unreliable readings are noted in the fault log.

Full-System Test

Run repeated cycles so the result shows reliability, not just one lucky squirt of water.

  • Input correctly controls the output through the controller or control circuit.
  • System waters only when the trigger condition is met.
  • System stops safely after the set time, wet reading, switch-off or teacher stop signal.
  • At least three full test cycles are recorded.
  • One improvement is made and retested after a fault or weak result.

Folio Evidence

  • Photo of safe test setup showing wet zone and dry electronics zone.
  • Labelled system diagram showing input, process, output and feedback.
  • Algorithm, flowchart or pseudocode showing watering decision and stop condition.
  • Test data table completed with at least three full-system cycles.
  • Fault log shows issue, likely cause, fix and retest result.
  • Final evaluation uses test evidence to judge safety, reliability, water efficiency and sustainability.
