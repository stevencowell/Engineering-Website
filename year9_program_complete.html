PROGRAM OVERVIEW

This program provides a comprehensive week-by-week scope and sequence for Year 9 Engineering, aligned with the Stage 5 Industrial Technology – Engineering syllabus. The program has been reorganized to focus each term on a single major project, ensuring deep learning and skill development.

TERM 1: WATER TOWER PROJECT

Focus: WHS, Structural Concepts, Design, Build and Test

Assessment: Formative (Learning Task - Due End of Term 1)

Website: year9_term1.html

Term 1 Overview

Term 1 introduces students to engineering through the Water Tower project. Students develop foundational skills in workplace health and safety, structural concepts, design planning, and systematic testing. This formative assessment builds essential skills for future summative tasks.

Key Learning Areas:

  • Workplace Health & Safety (WHS) and risk management
  • Structural concepts: forces, loads, trusses, tension, compression
  • Design and planning processes
  • Build, test, and evaluation procedures

TERM 3 - WEEK-BY-WEEK SCOPE AND SEQUENCE

Week 1: Fluid Power & Mechanisms Theory

Focus: Toolkit 1 - Fluid Power & Mechanisms

Website Link: Year9 T3 Modules/module1.html

Key Theory / Learning:
  • Pascal's Principle: Pressure transmission in fluids, P = F/A relationships
  • Hydraulic Systems: Closed systems, pressure multiplication, force amplification
  • Mechanical Advantage (MA): MA = Output Force / Input Force
  • Velocity Ratio (VR): VR = Input Distance / Output Distance
  • Efficiency: Efficiency = (MA / VR) × 100%, friction effects
  • Lever Systems: First, second, third class levers, fulcrum positions
  • Mechanisms: Linkages, pivots, motion transformation
Practical / Activities:
  • Theory: Pascal's principle calculations, MA/VR worksheet practice
  • Demonstrations: Hydraulic system demonstrations, lever mechanism examples
  • Experiments: Simple hydraulic system experiments, lever advantage measurements
  • Google Classroom: Complete mechanism analysis activities
Assessment & Evidence:
  • Pascal's principle calculations
  • MA/VR practice problems
  • Lever system analysis notes
  • Mechanism investigation notes
  • Formative: Understanding of fluid power and mechanism principles
Outcomes Focus:
  • IND5-2: Applies design principles (understanding mechanisms)

PROGRAM NOTES

Teaching Approach

  • Sequential Learning: Each toolkit builds on previous learning
  • Project-Based: Learning is contextualised through hands-on projects
  • Formative First: Term 1 and Term 3 provide formative learning opportunities
  • Summative Assessment: Term 2 (Task 1) and Term 4 (Task 2) are graded assessments
  • Skill Building: Skills develop progressively across terms

Differentiation Strategies

  • Extension: Advanced calculations, complex designs, extended testing
  • Support: Scaffolded worksheets, step-by-step guides, exemplars
  • Multiple Modalities: Visual (diagrams, videos), hands-on (builds, tests), written (folios, reports)

Safety Considerations

  • WHS First: All practical work preceded by WHS training
  • OnGuard Certification: Required before workshop access
  • SWMS: Required for all major operations
  • Supervision: Appropriate supervision ratios for all activities

Resource Requirements

  • Workshop: Hand tools, power tools, testing equipment
  • Materials: Timber, concrete materials, hydraulic components, gear components
  • ICT: Onshape accounts, computers, internet access
  • CAM: Laser cutter and/or CNC router (Term 4)
