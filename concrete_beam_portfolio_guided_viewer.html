Design Brief and Success Criteria

Design, cast, cure, and destructively test a reinforced concrete beam. Your folio needs to show the engineering decisions you made, the evidence you collected, and how your test results prove how well the beam performed.

Design Brief

  • Create a reinforced concrete beam using the class mould and material limits.
  • Place reinforcement on the tension face with suitable cover.
  • Plan the mix, casting sequence, curing method, and test method before building.

Success Criteria

  • Beam is labelled, cured for at least 7 days, and tested safely.
  • Folio includes drawings, photos, build log, test data, graph or table, and evaluation.
  • Evaluation explains first crack, failure load, failure mode, and specific improvements.

Evidence To Collect

  • Mould and reinforcement photos.
  • Mix ratio, casting date, curing record, and beam ID.
  • Load increments, deflection readings, first crack, failure load, and final damage photos.

Key Vocabulary

  • Compression, tension, cover, span, deflection, first crack, failure mode.
  • Composite material, reinforcement, curing, compaction, water-cement ratio.
