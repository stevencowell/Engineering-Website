Concrete Beam Project Instructions

Project brief

Design, cast, cure and destructively test a reinforced concrete beam, then evaluate how materials and design decisions affected performance.

  • Curing: Minimum 7 days before testing.
  • Reinforcement: Place steel on the tension face with 15-20 mm cover.
  • Mould: Build a 19 mm form-ply mould (ends 200 × 200; sides ~500 × 200; base form ply).
  • Test method: Load in measured steps; record deflection and observations.
  • Evidence: Photos + build log of mould, reinforcement, casting and curing.
  • Evaluation: Identify the failure mode and propose improvements.

What "good" looks like: accurate reinforcement placement, well-compacted mix, consistent test setup, and clear, labelled evidence in your folio.

Materials & evidence

  • Concrete ingredients (cement, sand, aggregate, water) and a planned mix ratio.
  • Reinforcement (wire/mesh/bar) plus spacers/supports for correct cover.
  • Mould (19 mm form ply): base 500 × 200; 2 sides 500 × 200; 2 ends 200 × 200.
  • Release agent, curing cover/wrap, and a beam ID label.

Hand in: a folio/report with design drawings, casting log, test results (table/graph), and evaluation.

Build steps

1
Interpret the brief

Identify the mould size, test span, reinforcement rules, and what data you must collect.

More tips
  • Highlight requirements: span, cross-section, load method, curing time, and safety.
  • Start a checklist and tick it off as you go.
  • Plan how you will label your beam (beam ID) and record dates.

Quick check: Have you listed every requirement and constraint?

2
Design beam geometry & reinforcement

Draw a cross-section showing dimensions, reinforcement position and cover.

More tips
  • For simply-supported testing, tension is usually on the bottom face.
  • Keep 15-20 mm cover to protect reinforcement and avoid cracking at the surface.
  • Predict a failure mode (tension, compression, shear) and justify your design.

Quick check: Does your drawing show dimensions and cover?

3
Plan mix & casting sequence

Calculate volume, choose a mix ratio, and write a step-by-step casting plan.

More tips
  • Measure consistently: record cement:sand:aggregate:water (and why you chose it).
  • Prepare tools before mixing (mould ready, reinforcement in place, PPE on).
  • Plan compaction (tapping/vibration) to reduce air voids.

Quick check: Is your casting plan clear enough for another group to follow?

4
Prepare mould & place reinforcement

Clean the mould, apply release (if used), then position reinforcement accurately.

More tips
  • Mould dimensions: ends 200 × 200; sides ~500 × 200; base 500 × 200 (all 19 mm form ply).
  • Assembly: screw/glue sides to the base, then fit the ends inside the side walls (internal length becomes ~462 mm because of 19 mm ply thickness).
  • If you need an internal length closer to 500 mm, cut the base and side panels ~538 mm long (500 + 2×19).
  • Use spacers/supports so reinforcement doesn't sink to the bottom.
  • Secure reinforcement so it can't move during placement and compaction.
  • Check cover at multiple points before pouring concrete.

Quick check: Is reinforcement locked in and centred with correct cover?

5
Mix, cast & cure

Mix safely, place concrete, compact, finish the surface, label, and cure for 7+ days.

More tips
  • Add water gradually; too much water weakens concrete and increases shrinkage.
  • Compact in layers to reduce voids and improve strength.
  • Keep concrete moist during curing (cover/wrap) to reduce cracking.

Quick check: Have you recorded the mix and curing start date?

6
Test, record & evaluate

Set the span, measure deflection, load in steps, then analyse the failure and improvements.

More tips
  • Keep supports level and load placement consistent (usually at mid-span).
  • Record: first crack load, failure load, deflection at each step, and observations.
  • Photograph the beam before, during and after failure; label the tension/compression faces.

Quick check: Do your results include a table (and graph if possible)?

Quick checks

  • Does your design show span, cross-section, and reinforcement layout?
  • Is reinforcement on the tension side with correct cover?
  • Is your mix ratio recorded and consistent?
  • Have you labelled the beam ID and curing time?

Task Overview

Design, cast, and test a reinforced concrete beam. Document your complete process from materials selection through casting, testing, and evaluation in a comprehensive project folio.

Key Requirements:

  • Understand materials properties and concrete as a composite material
  • Design beam geometry and reinforcement layout
  • Cast concrete beam following proper procedures
  • Conduct destructive testing after curing (minimum 7 days)
  • Analyse failure modes and evaluate performance

Term 2 Focus and Syllabus Alignment

Term 2 focuses on materials, concrete and composite materials: understanding material properties, concrete as a composite, and designing, casting, testing and evaluating concrete beams. The toolkits connect directly to the NSW Stage 5 syllabus, covering:

  • Materials & Corrosion: Material properties, corrosion mechanisms, protection methods, and material selection for structural applications.
  • Concrete & Composites: Concrete as a composite material, mix design, reinforcement, curing, and structural behaviour.
  • Design & Testing: Beam design principles, casting procedures, destructive testing, data analysis, and evaluation.

You will practice WHS and risk management, technical communication, testing and evaluation, data analysis, and iterative improvement throughout your concrete beam project.

Overview of Requirements

Term 2 focuses on materials, concrete and structural testing. Here's what you'll accomplish:

  • Materials & Corrosion Investigation: Explore material properties through corrosion experiments; understand why engineers choose specific materials; investigate protection methods.
  • Concrete & Composite Materials: Understand concrete as a composite material; learn about mix design, reinforcement, curing, and structural behaviour.
  • Concrete Beam Design & Planning: Design your beam (span, cross-section, reinforcement); plan mix proportions; develop casting sequence; prepare test procedures.
  • Concrete Beam Casting, Test & Evaluate: Cast beams following proper procedures; allow proper curing time; conduct destructive testing; analyse results and evaluate performance.
  • Comprehensive Folio: WHS documentation, design rationale, build log/photos, test data tables/graphs, analysis (predicted vs actual), and specific improvements.

Outcomes Alignment (IND5)

  • IND5-1: Apply WHS and manage risks during mixing, casting, curing and destructive testing.
  • IND5-2: Apply design principles to beam geometry and reinforcement decisions.
  • IND5-3: Select and use tools/equipment safely for formwork, mixing and test setup.
  • IND5-4: Select and justify materials (concrete mix, reinforcement, mould materials) for performance.
  • IND5-5: Communicate using drawings, tables/graphs and labelled photos in your folio.
  • IND5-8: Evaluate against criteria using test data and propose specific improvements.
  • IND5-9 & IND5-10: Discuss concrete technologies and impacts (environmental, sustainability) in your evaluation.

Assessment & Folio Checklist

Your Term 2 folio should be a clear record of your learning and your Concrete Beam project. Use this checklist to ensure you've captured the required evidence:

  • Safety Documentation: Completed SWMS and safety evidence for mixing/casting (cement dust and wet concrete), curing/storage, and destructive testing procedures.
  • Materials & Corrosion Notes: Material properties and selection (strength, ductility, toughness), corrosion basics, and practical protection methods (coatings, galvanising, isolation).
  • Concrete Design & Planning: Mix plan and rationale, reinforcement layout, mould plan, curing plan, and a clear build sequence.
  • Testing Data: Load test table (increments, first crack, failure load), observations/deflection notes, and labelled photos of crack development/failure.
  • Evaluation: Failure mode analysis (tension/compression/shear), what caused it, and at least one justified improvement (mix, compaction, reinforcement placement, curing time).
  • Communication: Neat data tables/graphs, correct engineering vocabulary, and references/sources where used.

Hands-on & Theory (Term 2 Program)

  • Materials properties: strength, ductility, toughness; why material choice matters in structures.
  • Corrosion: causes, prevention strategies, and how reinforcement protection relates to concrete cover.
  • Concrete as a composite: aggregates, cement paste, water ratio, curing, and how this affects strength.
  • Beam behaviour: bending stress, tension/compression faces, cracking, and reinforcement action.
  • Testing & data: load steps, deflection measurement, first crack vs failure, tables/graphs and analysis.

Resources & Differentiation

  • Materials: cement, sand, aggregate, water; reinforcement (wire/mesh/bar); moulds/formwork; spacers; curing covers.
  • Equipment: scales/buckets for measuring, mixing tubs, PPE (gloves/eye protection/dust masks), and test rig with load/deflection measurement.
  • Scaffolds: casting checklist, data table template (load vs deflection), and evaluation sentence starters for failure mode analysis.
  • Differentiation: optional design extensions (reinforcement variations, cross-section changes, curing comparisons) with controlled-variable testing.
