Term 2 Focus and Syllabus Alignment

Term 2 builds on your foundational knowledge from Term 1, introducing deeper engineering concepts through engaging, hands-on projects. The toolkits connect directly to the NSW Stage 5 syllabus, covering:

  • Structures: Tensegrity systems - understanding how tension and compression work together
  • Mechanisms & Control Systems: Renewable energy devices - exploring how we harness natural forces
  • Fluid Systems: Buoyancy and submarine design - applying physics principles to real-world challenges
  • Engineering Specialisation: CAD and Onshape - developing digital design skills

Throughout Term 2, you'll practice essential Stage 5 outcomes including risk management, clear technical communication, critical evaluation, materials selection, mechanical analysis, and sustainability thinking. Each toolkit builds these skills progressively.

Overview of Requirements

Term 2 challenges you to think like an engineer across multiple disciplines. Here's what you'll accomplish:

  • Tensegrity Structure: Design, build and document a tensegrity structure that demonstrates the balance between tension and compression. Learn to classify materials by their suitability for tension vs compression applications - a crucial engineering skill.
  • Renewable Energy Investigation: Explore alternate energy devices (wind, solar, hydro) through hands-on experiments. Record efficiency data and develop sustainability thinking - understanding how engineering solutions impact our environment.
  • Submarine Design Challenge: Create and test a bottle submarine that demonstrates ballast control, buoyancy principles, and density calculations. This project brings physics to life!
  • CAD Development: Develop parametric CAD models and technical drawings for tensegrity components or renewable energy parts. Learn professional design software used by engineers worldwide.
  • Comprehensive Folio: Maintain a detailed folio covering forces in equilibrium, material properties, energy transformations, and buoyancy data. Your folio demonstrates your engineering thinking and problem-solving process.

Outcomes Alignment (IND5)

  • IND5-1, IND5-3, IND5-4: WHS and SWMS for tensegrity builds, soldering/heat use, water testing and tool setup.
  • IND5-2, IND5-5, IND5-7: Iterative design of tensegrity and renewable devices with sketches, CAD, BOM and testing evidence.
  • IND5-6: Collaboration in small teams for energy experiments and submarine trials; shared risk controls and data collection.
  • IND5-8, IND5-9, IND5-10: Evaluate sustainability, embodied energy, material choice, buoyancy impacts and emerging renewable technologies.

Assessment & Folio Checklist

  • SWMS and safety evidence for cutting/drilling struts, tensioning cables, electrical testing and water-based labs.
  • Project report for tensegrity build with load/deflection measurements, materials rationale and photos.
  • Energy investigation logs (wind/solar/hydro) with voltage/current data, efficiency calculations and sustainability reflections.
  • Buoyancy experiments: density calculations, ballast tank notes, depth vs air-volume data and stability commentary.
  • CAD outputs: annotated sketches, Onshape screenshots, assembly steps and engineering drawings of key parts.

Hands-on & Theory (Term 2 Program)

  • Tensegrity prism build: tension/compression investigation, preload sequence and materials comparison.
  • Alternate energy challenges: wind turbine blade design, solar oven or panel mini-lab, hydro or wave car test.
  • Buoyancy labs: bottle submarine with ballast control, neutral buoyancy challenge, density measurements.
  • Forces and energy theory: tension vs compression, stress/strain basics, energy transformations and efficiency.
  • CAD & Onshape: model struts/caps, add cables, assemble tensegrity and generate drawings for the folio.

Resources & Differentiation

  • Materials: dowels/rod for struts, cord/wire for cables, DC motors/fans, solar cells, bottles/balloons/tubing, syringes and scales.
  • Digital: Onshape accounts, Google Classroom topics for CAD Tutorials, Alternate Energies, Submarine Design and Onshape Tutorials.
  • Scaffolds: vocab dictionaries for forces/energy/buoyancy, SWMS templates, sentence starters for data analysis and sustainability reflection.
  • Differentiation: hands-on demos, multilingual captions/translations, optional extension to multi-strut tensegrity or advanced CAD features.

Week Plan Snapshot (Guide)

  • W1: Introduce tensegrity concept; build mini model; review tension vs compression and material options.
  • W2: Full tensegrity prism build; preload and test; collect load/deflection evidence.
  • W3: Alternate energies intro; wind turbine blade experiments with voltage/current logging.
  • W4: Solar oven/panel task; sustainability discussion and embodied energy audit.
  • W5: Hydro or wave device build; compare outputs; folio update.
  • W6: Buoyancy theory; density calculations; start bottle submarine prototype.
  • W7: Submarine ballast experiments; dive/surface trials; record depth vs air volume.
  • W8: CAD introduction; model tensegrity strut/caps; explore variables and mates.
  • W9: Assemble CAD tensegrity or renewable component; produce drawings; folio consolidation.
  • W10: Reflection, quiz and catch-up; finalise reports, SWMS and evidence.
