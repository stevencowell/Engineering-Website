Term 3 Focus and Syllabus Alignment

Term 3 brings together everything you've learned so far, focusing on engineered mechanisms and structural systems. You'll investigate how forces, materials, and motion transformers combine to solve real engineering problems, then apply these concepts through exciting hands-on builds.

This term's projects include:

  • Water tower and concrete beam projects (building on Term 1 knowledge)
  • Hydraulic digger - exploring fluid power systems
  • Geared lolly dispenser - understanding mechanical advantage and motion control

Throughout Term 3, you'll continue developing core Stage 5 outcomes: risk management, mechanical analysis, materials selection, sustainability thinking, and technical communication. These skills provide the foundation for all your engineering projects.

Scope & Lesson Plans

  • Students build and test a water tower and concrete beam while recording loads, deflection and construction notes.
  • Investigate materials classification and corrosion; compare timber, metals, composites and protective treatments.
  • Design and construct a hydraulic digger, calculate force transfer and efficiency, and manage fluid-power risks.
  • Create a geared lolly dispenser with cams or ratchets; analyse gear ratios and motion control.
  • Use CAD/Onshape to draw assemblies, generate drawings and cost materials for each build.

Overview of Requirements

  • Complete WHS training, SWMS and risk assessments for cutting, drilling, mixing concrete and fluid power tasks.
  • Produce design reports with sketches, load calculations, materials testing results and safety evidence.
  • Undertake structural analysis: forces, loads, truss behaviour, mechanical advantage and simple machine maths.
  • Calculate fluid power forces, velocity ratio and efficiency for hydraulic systems using syringe data.
  • Maintain costing spreadsheets, technical drawings and research tasks on careers and sustainability impacts.

Outcomes Alignment

  • EGT5-SAF-01: Apply risk management, WHS training and SWMS to workshop, fluid power and testing activities.
  • EGT5-MEA-01: Analyse mechanical systems through forces, loads, gear ratios, mechanical advantage and efficiency.
  • EGT5-USE-01: Select and apply materials appropriately, considering corrosion, protection and structural performance.
  • EGT5-COM-01: Communicate engineering ideas with sketches, CAD, drawings, reports and organised folios.
  • EGT5-ENV-01: Evaluate sustainability and environmental impacts across materials, builds and project choices.

Assessment & Folio Checklist

  • Water tower/concrete beam: design brief, SWMS, load test data, mix ratios, reinforcement notes and evaluation.
  • Hydraulic digger: lever layout sketches, syringe calculations, build photos, test data tables and reflections.
  • Geared lolly dispenser: gear ratio calculations, cam/ratchet notes, jamming tests and user feedback.
  • Folio requirements: design brief, planning documents, risk assessments, detailed theory notes, CAD drawings, testing data, evaluation and reflection.

Hands-on & Theory (Term 3 Program)

  • Weeks 1-2: WHS training, intro to engineering fields, materials classification, forces and loads, paper/balsa towers.
  • Weeks 3-4: Build and test water towers and concrete beams; corrosion investigations and damp proof course demo; begin materials research report.
  • Weeks 5-6: Simple machines, MA/VR/efficiency calculations; design and start hydraulic digger; apply Pascal's principle and syringe maths.
  • Weeks 7-8: Build geared lolly dispenser; explore gear trains, cams and ratchets; costing spreadsheets; CAD/Onshape drawings and reports.

Resources & Differentiation

  • Materials: timber, metal rods, syringes, plastic tubing, screws, adhesives, gear kits, and access to 3D printing or laser cutting.
  • Digital: Onshape accounts for CAD, spreadsheets for costing and test data, templates for SWMS and risk assessments.
  • Extensions: design a four-arm hydraulic digger, add electronics or sensors, or prototype alternate gear/cam solutions.
  • Differentiation: scaffolded calculations, labelled diagrams, peer pairing for builds, and step-by-step videos for tool setup.
