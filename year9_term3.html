Hydraulic Digger Project Instructions

Project brief

Design and build a tabletop hydraulic digger using syringes, tubing and lever linkages. Then test performance and evaluate how syringe sizing and lever geometry affected force and movement.

  • Functions: 3 separate hydraulic circuits (boom, dipper, bucket).
  • Reach: Meet the class reach target (e.g., 300-400 mm).
  • Lifting: Meet the class lift target (e.g., 50-100 g) with controlled motion.
  • Hydraulics: Leak-free, secured syringes/tubing, and de-aired for smooth control.
  • Evidence: Photos/video + build log; calculations for syringe areas and MA/VR.

What \"good\" looks like: smooth motion with minimal air, repeatable tests, clear calculations, and specific improvements linked to theory.

Materials & evidence

  • Syringes (e.g., 5 mL, 10 mL, 20 mL) + tubing, water, and clamps/zip ties.
  • Frame materials: timber/ply, fasteners, brackets, pivots (bolts/dowels), and bucket.
  • Testing items: small masses, ruler/tape, spring balance (if used), and a clear test setup.

Hand in: a folio/report with design sketches, lever/syringe sizing notes, build photos, test data tables and evaluation.

Build steps

1
Interpret the brief

Confirm reach, lifting target, number of functions, and what test data you must collect.

More tips
  • Write a checklist: reach, lift, smooth control, leak-free, evidence required.
  • Decide how you will measure reach (horizontal distance) and lift (mass/force).
  • Plan safety: cutting/drilling, water spills, pinch points, and syringe handling.

Quick check: Do you know exactly how your digger will be tested?

2
Design the arm geometry

Sketch the base, boom, dipper and bucket with pivot points and cylinder attachment locations.

More tips
  • Link geometry to behaviour: cylinder closer to fulcrum increases force but reduces movement.
  • Plan bucket motion so it curls and dumps without binding.
  • Keep pivots aligned and arms stiff to reduce friction and twisting.

Quick check: Does your sketch show pivots and cylinder mounts?

3
Select syringe sizes and layout

Choose input/output syringes for each function and plan hose routing.

More tips
  • Record syringe sizes and calculate piston areas (show units).
  • Bigger output syringes increase force but reduce travel; balance force vs reach.
  • Keep hoses short, smooth and protected from sharp edges.

Quick check: Have you predicted the force/travel trade-off?

4
Build the frame and arms

Cut, drill and assemble the base, boom and dipper with accurate pivots.

More tips
  • Drill square holes; use spacers/washers so parts move freely without wobble.
  • Test dry movement first (no hydraulics) to check for binding.
  • Reinforce joints where bending occurs (especially boom-base pivot).

Quick check: Do the arms move smoothly by hand?

5
Plumb and de-air the hydraulics

Fill syringes/tubing with water, connect circuits, and cycle until motion is smooth.

More tips
  • Fill slowly to avoid bubbles; keep plungers within safe travel.
  • Secure syringes so they don't rotate or flex under load.
  • Fix leaks immediately: re-seat tubing, tighten clamps, replace damaged parts.

Quick check: Is motion smooth (not spongy) and leak-free?

6
Test, record and evaluate

Measure reach and lifting capacity; record input/output motion; compare predicted vs actual MA/VR.

More tips
  • Use a consistent setup: same start position, same measurement points, same loads.
  • Record a table of results (and a short video showing each function).
  • Suggest specific improvements (e.g., change lever ratio or syringe pairing) and explain the trade-offs.

Quick check: Do you have enough evidence to justify your evaluation?

Quick checks

  • Do you have 3 working circuits (boom, dipper, bucket)?
  • Are syringes secured and hoses routed safely (no kinks/leaks)?
  • Have you removed air (smooth, repeatable motion)?
  • Have you recorded syringe sizes/areas and test data (reach, lift, movement)?

Term 3 Focus: Hydraulic Digger Project

Term 3 focuses on fluid power and mechanisms through the Hydraulic Digger project. You will learn about Pascal's principle, lever linkages, mechanical advantage, and velocity ratio while building and testing a functional hydraulic control system.

Key goals for this term:

  • Understand fluid power systems and Pascal's principle (P = F/A).
  • Design and build a hydraulic digger with lever linkages.
  • Calculate and measure mechanical advantage (MA) and velocity ratio (VR).
  • Conduct systematic testing and compare predicted vs measured performance.
  • Document your project in a comprehensive folio.

Overview of Requirements

Term 3 focuses on fluid power and mechanisms through the Hydraulic Digger project. Here's what you'll accomplish:

  • Fluid Power Theory: Understand Pascal's principle, hydraulic vs pneumatic systems, and pressure calculations.
  • Lever Linkages: Design and build lever systems that convert small movements into large forces.
  • Hydraulic Digger Build: Construct a tabletop digger with hydraulic control system, removing air and ensuring proper operation.
  • Testing & Analysis: Measure effort, travel, and load; calculate MA and VR; compare predicted vs measured performance.
  • Comprehensive Folio: Document design, build process, test data, analysis, and evaluation.

Assessment & Folio Checklist

Your Term 3 folio should clearly show your design thinking, build process, test data, and evaluation for the Hydraulic Digger project.

  • Safety Documentation: Completed SWMS and safety evidence for cutting/drilling, syringes/tubing, pinch points, and safe testing procedures.
  • Design Evidence: Design brief analysis, labelled sketches, linkage layout, syringe sizing choices, and a build sequence (step-by-step).
  • Fluid Power Calculations: Pascal's principle work shown (P = F/A), syringe areas, predicted forces, and clear units.
  • Testing Data: Measurements of effort/load and travel; MA and VR calculations; predicted vs measured comparison table.
  • Evidence Capture: Photos of key build stages, plumbing/de-airing, final assembly, and testing setup; optional short video of operation.
  • Evaluation: What worked, what failed first (leaks, air, friction, flex), and at least one justified improvement you made (or would make).

Week Plan Snapshot (Guide)

  • Weeks 1-2: Complete Toolkit 1 (Fluid Power & Mechanisms theory) and Toolkit 2 (Hydraulic Digger Design). Understand Pascal's principle, lever systems, and design your digger.
  • Weeks 2-4: Build your hydraulic digger (Toolkit 3). Construct frame, install hydraulic system, plumb syringes, remove air, test basic operation.
  • Weeks 4-6: Testing and evaluation (Toolkit 4). Measure forces, calculate MA/VR, compare predicted vs measured, iterate on design improvements.
  • Weeks 7-8: Complete folio documentation. Collate photos, data tables, analysis, and reflection. Polish design drawings and technical communication.
  • Weeks 9-10: Finalise folio and submit. Review and consolidation of fluid power and mechanism concepts.
