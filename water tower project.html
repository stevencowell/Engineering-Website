Task 1 – Water Tower

Student guide. This is the “do this, not that” version.

Work in groups of 4
Design + build + test
Stay under the mass limit

The Brief (what your tower must do)

Build a tower that holds heavy weight safely and efficiently using the supplied material.

  • Height
    At least 300 mm tall.
  • Mass
    No more than 60 g.
  • Load
    Must hold at least 25 kg.
  • Time
    Must hold that load for at least 5 seconds.
  • Top
    Top must support a 300 mm square board during testing.
What does “good” look like?
  • Triangles/bracing so it doesn’t rack sideways.
  • Neat, strong joints (most failures happen here).
  • Clear load path: top → legs/columns → base.
  • Stable base so it doesn’t wobble.

Materials you must use

  • 4 mm square cross-section plywood (structural members supplied).
  • 300 mm square board sits on top during testing.
  • Cast iron weights placed on top during testing.
Mass planning tip (optional)

There’s a mass guide in the project info (~10.6 g per metre). Use it to estimate your timber length so you don’t blow the 60 g limit.

What you hand in

  • Your tower (built and ready to test).
  • Your folio/report with evidence of your process.
  • Testing results (mass, load held, hold time).
How you’re marked (optional)
  • Project / build quality: 70%
  • Load-to-mass ratio: 10%
  • Report/folio: 20%

Build steps

  1. Sketch 2–3 ideas

    Pick a design that is strong but light. Triangles help.

  2. Draw your final design (with measurements)

    Show height and where the top board will sit.

  3. Plan your cuts BEFORE you cut

    Long pieces are heavy and can buckle.

  4. Build it square and straight

    Use clamps/jigs. Wobbly towers die early.

  5. Check stability

    Does it rock? Are joints tight? Is the top platform stable?

  6. Test and record results

    Record mass (g), load (kg), hold time (s), and what failed (if it failed).
