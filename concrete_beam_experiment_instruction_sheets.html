Class Test Plan

Each beam setup is made twice. The repeated beam checks whether the result was fair or just a one-off.

Groups Beam setup Main variable
1 and 2 A - Plain concrete No reinforcement, standard mix
3 and 4 B - Steel rods low Steel near the bottom, standard mix
5 and 6 C - Steel rods high Steel near the top, standard mix
7 and 8 D - Mesh reinforcement Steel mesh inside beam, standard mix
9 and 10 E - Changed concrete mix Leaner mix, no reinforcement

Keep These The Same

  • Use the same mould size unless your teacher says otherwise.
  • Use the same casting method.
  • Use the same curing time.
  • Use the same test setup.
  • Label your beam clearly before it cures.

Concrete Mix Ratios

Use the same cup, scoop, or container for every part. These ratios are by volume, not weight.

Setup Dry mix ratio Water rule
A, B, C, D Standard mix: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate Add water slowly until the mix is workable but not runny.
E Changed mix: 1 cement : 3 sand : 3 aggregate Use the same water rule. Record how much water was added.

Example standard batch: 1 cup cement, 2 cups sand, 3 cups aggregate. Example changed batch: 1 cup cement, 3 cups sand, 3 cups aggregate.

Safety First

  • Wear safety glasses, gloves, and a dust mask when mixing dry cement.
  • Do not breathe in cement dust.
  • Wash concrete off your skin straight away.
  • Keep fingers clear when testing beams.
  • Only test when your teacher says the area is ready.

Every Group Must Record

  • Group number and beam setup letter.
  • Beam dimensions.
  • Concrete mix used.
  • How much water was added.
  • Reinforcement type and position.
  • Photos of mould, reinforcement, casting, curing, and testing.
  • Maximum load before failure.
  • Where and how the beam cracked.

Setup A - Plain Concrete Beam

Groups 1 and 2

Goal

Make a plain concrete beam with no reinforcement. This is the control beam used for comparison.

Sequence

  1. Collect one clean mould.
  2. Write your group number and Setup A on the beam label.
  3. Lightly oil or line the mould if instructed.
  4. Mix the standard concrete mix: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate.
  5. Add water slowly and record how much you used.
  6. Fill the mould in layers.
  7. Tap or vibrate the mould gently to remove air pockets.
  8. Level the top with a trowel or straight edge.
  9. Place the label where it can be seen after curing.
  10. Move the beam to the curing area.

Check Before You Finish

  • No steel rods.
  • No mesh.
  • Standard concrete mix only: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate.
  • Water amount has been recorded.
  • Beam is labelled.

Prediction

Will this beam be stronger or weaker than reinforced beams? Why?

Setup B - Steel Rods Near Bottom

Groups 3 and 4

Goal

Test how well steel rods work when placed near the bottom of the beam.

Sequence

  1. Collect one clean mould and the steel rods provided.
  2. Write your group number and Setup B on the beam label.
  3. Place small spacers in the bottom of the mould if provided.
  4. Place the steel rods lengthways near the bottom of the beam.
  5. Keep the rods away from the edges so concrete can cover them.
  6. Mix the standard concrete mix: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate.
  7. Add water slowly and record how much you used.
  8. Carefully fill the mould without moving the rods.
  9. Tap or vibrate the mould gently to remove air pockets.
  10. Level, label, and move the beam to the curing area.

Check Before You Finish

  • Steel rods are near the bottom.
  • Rods run along the length of the beam.
  • Rods are covered by concrete.
  • Standard concrete mix only: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate.
  • Water amount has been recorded.

Prediction

Why might steel near the bottom help when the load pushes down from above?

Setup C - Steel Rods Near Top

Groups 5 and 6

Goal

Test what happens when the steel rods are placed near the top of the beam instead of the bottom.

Sequence

  1. Collect one clean mould and the steel rods provided.
  2. Write your group number and Setup C on the beam label.
  3. Mix the standard concrete mix: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate.
  4. Add water slowly and record how much you used.
  5. Add a shallow layer of concrete to the mould first.
  6. Place the steel rods lengthways near the top of the beam.
  7. Keep the rods away from the top surface and side edges.
  8. Fill the rest of the mould carefully.
  9. Make sure the rods stay near the top and do not sink.
  10. Tap or vibrate the mould gently to remove air pockets.
  11. Level, label, and move the beam to the curing area.

Check Before You Finish

  • Steel rods are near the top.
  • Rods run along the length of the beam.
  • Rods are fully covered by concrete.
  • Standard concrete mix only: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate.
  • Water amount has been recorded.

Prediction

Do you think this will be stronger or weaker than Setup B? Explain your reason.

Setup D - Mesh Reinforced Beam

Groups 7 and 8

Goal

Test whether mesh reinforcement improves the beam strength compared with plain concrete.

Sequence

  1. Collect one clean mould and one piece of mesh.
  2. Write your group number and Setup D on the beam label.
  3. Check that the mesh fits inside the mould.
  4. Place the mesh inside the beam, slightly below the middle if instructed.
  5. Keep the mesh away from the bottom, top, and side edges.
  6. Mix the standard concrete mix: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate.
  7. Add water slowly and record how much you used.
  8. Fill the mould carefully so the mesh stays in position.
  9. Tap or vibrate the mould gently to remove air pockets.
  10. Level, label, and move the beam to the curing area.

Check Before You Finish

  • Mesh is inside the beam, not touching the mould.
  • Mesh is covered by concrete.
  • Standard concrete mix only: 1 cement : 2 sand : 3 aggregate.
  • Water amount has been recorded.
  • Beam is labelled.

Prediction

How might mesh change the way the beam cracks?

Setup E - Changed Concrete Mix

Groups 9 and 10

Goal

Test how changing the concrete mix affects beam strength. This beam has no reinforcement.

Sequence

  1. Collect one clean mould.
  2. Write your group number and Setup E on the beam label.
  3. Use the changed mix ratio: 1 cement : 3 sand : 3 aggregate.
  4. Measure the materials carefully. Do not guess.
  5. Mix until the concrete looks even.
  6. Add water slowly. The mix should be workable, not runny.
  7. Record how much water you used.
  8. Fill the mould in layers.
  9. Tap or vibrate the mould gently to remove air pockets.
  10. Level, label, and move the beam to the curing area.

Check Before You Finish

  • No steel rods.
  • No mesh.
  • Changed mix ratio has been recorded: 1 cement : 3 sand : 3 aggregate.
  • Water amount has been recorded.
  • Beam is labelled.

Test result notes

Testing Day Sequence

  1. Collect your cured beam and check the label.
  2. Measure and record the beam dimensions.
  3. Set the beam on the supports exactly as shown by your teacher.
  4. Load the beam slowly and safely.
  5. Record the maximum load before failure.
  6. Photograph the crack pattern.
  7. Compare your result with the group that made the same setup.
  8. Explain whether the repeat test was similar or different.
