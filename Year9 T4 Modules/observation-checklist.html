Testing & Observation Checklist

Safety, measurements, evidence capture for the Geared Lolly Dispenser project

Safety Prep

  • SWMS and machine setup checks completed (laser/CNC/drill press/sanding) and PPE confirmed.
  • Moving parts safe: pinch points identified, guards installed where possible, and hands kept clear during testing.
  • Dispenser secured on a stable surface; no loose fasteners; shafts/axles aligned and retained.
  • Test area clear and consistent: same lolly type/size, same loading method, and a clear “stop” procedure if jams occur.

Build & QA Checks

  • Gear mesh checked: correct centre distance, minimal wobble, and smooth rotation without binding.
  • Cam/ratchet alignment checked (if used): follower contact consistent; ratchet prevents reverse rotation.
  • Hopper/gate path clear: lollies feed reliably without catching; gate clears one lolly per cycle.
  • Fasteners/adhesives set and safe: nothing rubs on rotating parts; guards don’t interfere with motion.

Measurement & Data

  • Record gear teeth counts and calculate predicted gear ratio / turns-per-dispense (show working).
  • Run a measured test set (e.g. 20 cycles) and record dispenses vs turns and any jams/misfeeds.
  • Complete reliability testing (50–100+ cycles) with a jam log (what happened + suspected cause).
  • Compare predicted vs measured performance (table + short explanation for differences: friction, slip, tolerance, dwell).

Evidence Capture

  • Photos: CAD screenshots/drawings, cut parts, assembly stages, guards, and final dispenser.
  • Photos/video: testing setup plus at least one successful full dispense sequence.
  • Folio organised: safety section, CAD pack, manufacturing notes (kerf/fit), testing data tables/graphs.
  • Reflection drafted: what failed first, what you changed, and one improvement to increase reliability or safety.
