Goals for this activity

In this task you will be expected to:

  • Maintain safe work practices using more complex tools, equipment, and processes (CAD-CAM, laser/CNC, assembly).
  • Produce a high-quality engineering project based on Term 4 concepts (gear systems, mechanisms, CAD, reliability testing).
  • Use CAD/Onshape and other technologies to communicate and refine your design.
  • Document the complete development of your project in a detailed engineering folio.
  • Evaluate the performance, efficiency, and environmental impact of your solution.

Task Outline

This is the second major engineering project for the course. It focuses on the Geared Lolly Dispenser project completed during Term 4.

Geared Lolly Dispenser Project (Summative, marked) - Term 4

  • Project: Geared Lolly Dispenser - Design, build, and test a functional geared dispenser with compound gears, cams, and ratchets.
  • Timing:
    • Weeks 1-2: Gear systems & mechanisms theory, design (Toolkits 1-2)
    • Weeks 2-4: Build & manufacture (Toolkit 3)
    • Weeks 4-6: Testing, reliability & evaluation (Toolkit 4)
    • Week 6: Folio submission (includes all three projects)
  • Evidence required (full folio):
    • Evidence Required:
      • Brief & specs; CAD/Onshape screenshots; materials/tools; operations plan
      • WHS: hazards/controls for drilling/cutting/assembly/testing; PPE used
      • Build log with photos (key stages)
      • Test data: gear teeth counts; theoretical vs measured turns-per-candy; reliability/jam log (50-100+ cycles); notes on cam/ratchet timing
      • Data tables and graphs showing reliability, failure modes, before/after improvements
      • Analysis: compare predicted vs measured ratio; identify causes of mismatch
      • Improvements: at least two specific adjustments (e.g., change gear pair, adjust gate/hopper, spacing/guarding)
      • Reflection linking to real devices and safe, reliable operation

Criteria

In this task you will be assessed on how well you:

  • Apply WHS practices and work safely and cooperatively in the workshop.
  • Produce a complex, functional engineering project demonstrating good workmanship and accuracy.
  • Use CAD/Onshape and other communication techniques to clearly present your design.
  • Document your project thoroughly in a well-structured folio.
  • Evaluate your project's performance, efficiency, and impact, and propose realistic improvements.
