Goals for this activity

In this task you will be expected to:

  • Maintain safe work practices using more complex tools, equipment, and processes.
  • Produce a high-quality engineering project based on Term 2 concepts (materials, concrete as a composite material, and concrete beam design and testing).
  • Use appropriate communication techniques to document your design, materials selection, and testing procedures.
  • Document the complete development of your project in a detailed engineering folio.
  • Evaluate the performance, efficiency, and environmental impact of your solution.

Task Outline

This is the second major engineering project for the course. It is based on your Term 2 work on the Concrete Beam project. You will design, construct, and test a concrete beam and document your work in a comprehensive folio.

Project requirements

This task focuses on the Concrete Beam project completed during Term 2. Your folio should document the complete process from materials selection through casting, testing, and evaluation.

Concrete Beam Project (Summative, marked) - Term 2

  • Project: Concrete Beam Casting & Destructive Test - Design, cast, and test a reinforced concrete beam.
  • Timing: Complete the casting during Term 2, Weeks 2-4. Test after proper curing (Weeks 5-6). Submit your folio in Term 4, Week 6.
  • Evidence required (full folio):
    • Brief & criteria; design sketches/plan showing beam geometry, reinforcement layout; materials/tools list; casting sequence.
    • WHS: hazards/controls for concrete mixing, casting, and testing; PPE used.
    • Build log with photos (key stages: formwork preparation, reinforcement placement, mixing, casting, curing).
    • Test data: beam span; mix ratio (cement:sand:aggregate:water); reinforcement type and placement; cure time; load at first crack; load at failure; note tension/compression face; photo of failure.
    • Data table and graph showing load vs deflection (if applicable).
    • Analysis: compare expected vs actual performance; identify failure causes (tension failure, compression failure, shear failure).
    • Evaluation: assess against design criteria (strength, durability, material efficiency).
    • Improvements: at least two specific improvements (e.g., change reinforcement type/placement, adjust mix ratio, modify geometry).
    • Reflection linking concrete properties to performance and real-world applications.

Criteria

In this task you will be assessed on how well you:

  • Apply WHS practices and work safely and cooperatively in the workshop.
  • Produce a complex, functional engineering project demonstrating good workmanship and accuracy.
  • Use appropriate communication techniques to clearly present your design, materials selection, and testing procedures.
  • Document your project thoroughly in a well-structured folio.
  • Evaluate your project’s performance, efficiency, and impact, and propose realistic improvements.
