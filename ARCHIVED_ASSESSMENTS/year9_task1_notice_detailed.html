Goals for this activity

In this task you will be expected to:

  • Understand and apply safe work practices in a practical engineering workshop environment.
  • Use a range of materials, tools, and techniques in the production of an engineering project linked to structures, structural concepts, and design principles.
  • Communicate effectively using verbal, graphical, and written methods in a project folio.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of how engineering technologies impact society and the environment.
  • Apply principles of design in the development, production, testing, and evaluation of an engineering project.

Task Outline

This assessment task is based on your Term 1 work on the Water Tower project. You will complete a practical engineering project and document it in a detailed project report (folio).

Project requirements

This task focuses on the Water Tower project completed during Term 1 (Weeks 2-5). Your folio should document the complete process from design through testing and evaluation.

Water Tower Project (Summative, marked) - Term 1, Weeks 2-5

  • Project: Water Tower Challenge - Design, build, and test a structural water tower.
  • Timing: Complete the build during Term 1, Weeks 2-5. Submit your folio in Term 2, Week 7.
  • Evidence required (full folio):
    • Brief & criteria; design sketches/plan; materials/tools list; sequence of operations.
    • WHS: hazards/controls, PPE used.
    • Build log with photos (key stages: planning, jointing, assembly, finishing).
    • Test data: tower height, mass, load at failure, failure mode, deflection measurements.
    • Data table and graph showing load vs deflection (if applicable).
    • Analysis: compare expected vs actual performance; identify failure causes (buckling, joint failure, material failure).
    • Evaluation: assess against design criteria (function, safety, aesthetics, sustainability).
    • Reflection: PMI analysis with at least one specific improvement.

Criteria

In this task you will be assessed on how well you:

  • Apply safe work practices in the production and testing of your project.
  • Produce a quality, functional engineering project using appropriate tools, materials, and processes.
  • Communicate and document the development of your project in a clear and organised folio.
  • Apply design principles and engineering concepts to solve problems and make decisions.
  • Evaluate the success of your project using the PMI method and testing data.
