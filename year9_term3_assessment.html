Goals for this activity

In this task you will be expected to:

  • Understand and apply fluid power principles (Pascal's principle, pressure, force, area relationships).
  • Design and build a hydraulic digger with lever linkages demonstrating mechanical advantage.
  • Calculate and measure mechanical advantage (MA) and velocity ratio (VR).
  • Conduct systematic testing and compare predicted vs measured performance.
  • Document your project thoroughly in a well-structured folio.

Task Outline

This assessment task is based on your Term 3 work on the Hydraulic Digger project. You will design, build, and test a hydraulic control system and document it in a detailed project report (folio).

Hydraulic Digger Project – Term 3, Weeks 1-4

  • Project: Hydraulic Digger - Design, build, and test a tabletop hydraulic digger with lever linkages.
  • Timing:
    • Week 1: Fluid power & mechanisms theory (Toolkit 1)
    • Week 2: Digger design & planning (Toolkit 2)
    • Weeks 2-3: Build (Toolkit 3)
    • Weeks 3-4: Testing & evaluation (Toolkit 4)
    • End of Term 3: Folio submission (formative assessment)
  • Evidence required (full folio):
    • Brief & criteria; design sketches/plan showing lever geometry, syringe sizing, linkage design; materials/tools list; build sequence.
    • WHS: hazards/controls for hydraulic systems, fluid handling, testing; PPE used.
    • Build log with photos (key stages: frame assembly, hydraulic system setup, air bleeding, linkage mounting, pre-test inspection).
    • Test data: syringe sizes/areas, lever lengths, predicted MA/VR, measured input force, load lifted, stroke distances, efficiency calculations.
    • Data tables and graphs comparing predicted vs measured MA/VR.
    • Analysis: compare expected vs actual performance; identify causes of differences (friction, leakage, alignment).
    • Evaluation: assess against design criteria (function, efficiency, reliability, safety).
    • Improvements: at least two specific improvements (e.g., adjust lever ratios, change syringe sizes, improve alignment).
    • Reflection linking fluid power principles to real-world applications.

Criteria

In this task you will be assessed on how well you:

  • Apply WHS practices and work safely with hydraulic systems and fluids.
  • Produce a functional hydraulic digger demonstrating understanding of fluid power principles.
  • Use appropriate communication techniques to clearly present your design and testing procedures.
  • Document your project thoroughly in a well-structured folio.
  • Evaluate your project's performance, efficiency, and impact, and propose realistic improvements.
