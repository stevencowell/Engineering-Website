Safety, measurements, evidence capture for the Concrete Beam project

Safety Prep

  • SWMS reviewed and controls in place for mixing/casting and destructive testing.
  • PPE on: safety glasses, enclosed shoes, dust mask for cement, gloves for wet concrete, apron/long sleeves as required.
  • Work area clear; mould/fixtures stable; lifting and pinch points identified; first aid kit accessible; supervisor aware.
  • Testing machine/setup checked: supports aligned, load point centred, guards/barriers set, clear stop protocol agreed.

Measurement & Data

  • Record beam dimensions, reinforcement layout (size/position), and beam mass before testing.
  • Record mix details used (cement/sand/aggregate ratios, water amount) and curing time (days).
  • Capture load increments and total load at first crack and at failure (include photos at key stages).
  • Note failure mode: tension cracking, compression crushing, shear failure, reinforcement slip, or poor bond/voids.

Evidence Capture

  • Photos: mould/prep, reinforcement in mould, pour/finish, curing setup, final beam, test setup, failure close-up.
  • Video: side angle during loading showing deflection/crack development and the failure moment (if safe to record).
  • Data table updated (load vs observations) and one simple graph prepared (load vs stage or load vs deflection if measured).
  • Reflection drafted: what failed first, why it failed, and one change to improve strength/reliability (mix, reinforcement, curing, compaction).
