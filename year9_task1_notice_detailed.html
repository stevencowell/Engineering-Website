Goals for this activity

In this task you will be expected to:

  • Understand and apply safe work practices in a practical engineering workshop environment.
  • Use a range of materials, tools, and techniques in the production of an engineering project linked to structures, materials, corrosion, and concrete.
  • Communicate effectively using verbal, graphical, and written methods in a project folio.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of how engineering technologies impact society and the environment.
  • Apply principles of design in the development, production, testing, and evaluation of an engineering project.

Task Outline

This assessment task is based on your Term 1 and early Term 2 work on structures, materials, corrosion, and concrete as a composite material. You will complete a practical engineering project (such as a water tower structure and/or concrete beam test) and document the process in a detailed project report (folio).

Project requirements

  • Produce a functional engineering project that demonstrates:
    • Safe and correct use of tools and equipment.
    • Appropriate material selection and justification.
    • Application of structural concepts (forces, load paths, triangulation, compression, tension).
  • Complete a project folio that includes:
    • Project brief and design ideas (sketches and notes).
    • Planning documentation (sequence of operations, tools and materials list).
    • WHS considerations (hazard identification, basic controls, PPE used).
    • Photographs or drawings of key stages of construction and testing.
    • Written explanation of materials chosen and their properties.
    • Test results (for example, corrosion observations or concrete beam testing outcomes).
    • PMI evaluation of the finished project.

Criteria

In this task you will be assessed on how well you:

  • Apply safe work practices in the production and testing of your project.
  • Produce a quality, functional engineering project using appropriate tools, materials, and processes.
  • Communicate and document the development of your project in a clear and organised folio.
  • Apply design principles and engineering concepts to solve problems and make decisions.
  • Evaluate the success of your project using the PMI method and testing data.
