# Teaching Strategies Mapping: Term 1 & Term 2 Toolkits

## Overview
This document maps where specific teaching strategies are addressed across the Year 9 Engineering Technology Term 1 and Term 2 toolkits.

---

## TERM 1 TOOLKITS

### Toolkit 1: WHS & Risk Control

#### **Explicit Instruction**
- **Location**: Module 1 HTML - "Hand Tools - Safe Use", "Power Tools - Core Rules" sections
- **Evidence**: Step-by-step rules and procedures clearly stated (e.g., "Only use tools you have been trained and approved to use")
- **Implementation**: Direct teaching of safety procedures before practical work

#### **Scaffolded Learning**
- **Location**: Module 1 HTML - "Risk Management & Hierarchy of Control" section
- **Evidence**: Hierarchy presented from most to least effective (Elimination → Substitution → Engineering → Administrative → PPE)
- **Implementation**: Structured framework for risk assessment thinking

#### **Interactive Q&A Format**
- **Location**: Module 1 HTML - "WHS Q&A" section (8 questions)
- **Evidence**: Toggle buttons reveal answers, promoting self-checking
- **Implementation**: Students can test understanding independently

#### **Visual Learning**
- **Location**: Module 1 HTML - Hierarchy of Controls diagram image
- **Evidence**: Visual representation of control hierarchy
- **Implementation**: Supports visual learners and reinforces concepts

#### **Formative Assessment**
- **Location**: Module 1 HTML - "Activities & Evidence" section
- **Evidence**: OnGuard completion, SWMS drafts, workshop safety sign design
- **Implementation**: Multiple checkpoints before summative assessment

#### **Real-World Connections**
- **Location**: Module 1 HTML - "Why This Matters" section
- **Evidence**: Links to professional engineering habits and career applications
- **Implementation**: Contextualizes learning beyond classroom

## CROSS-CUTTING STRATEGIES ACROSS ALL TOOLKITS

### **1. Progressive Complexity**
- **Term 1**: Simple structures → Complex analysis
- **Term 2**: Basic tensegrity → Compound mechanisms
- **Implementation**: Skills build systematically across terms

### **2. Integration of Theory and Practice**
- **All Toolkits**: Key Notes sections provide theory, Activities sections apply it
- **Implementation**: Every toolkit links concepts to hands-on work

### **3. Multiple Assessment Modes**
- **All Toolkits**: Formative (activities, Q&A) + Summative (folios, reports)
- **Implementation**: Ongoing feedback with final demonstration

### **4. Safety Integration**
- **All Toolkits**: WHS embedded in every practical activity
- **Implementation**: Safety thinking becomes habitual

### **5. Sustainability Thinking**
- **All Toolkits**: Environmental considerations in material choices and design
- **Implementation**: EGT5-ENV-01 addressed throughout

### **6. Digital Literacy**
- **Term 1**: PBS simulators, OnGuard, Google Classroom
- **Term 2**: Onshape CAD, spreadsheets, digital documentation
- **Implementation**: ICT skills developed progressively

### **7. Collaborative Learning**
- **All Toolkits**: Pair/group work on builds, testing, investigations
- **Implementation**: Teamwork skills developed alongside technical skills

### **8. Metacognitive Development**
- **All Toolkits**: Evaluation sections, reflection prompts, improvement suggestions
- **Implementation**: Students learn to assess and improve their own work
