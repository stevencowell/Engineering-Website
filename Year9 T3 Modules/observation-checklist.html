Testing & Observation Checklist

Safety, measurements, evidence capture for the Hydraulic Digger project

Safety Prep

  • SWMS reviewed and controls in place for cutting/drilling, hot glue, syringes/tubing and pinch points.
  • PPE on: safety glasses and enclosed shoes; hair and loose clothing secured.
  • Test area clear; moving parts guarded where possible; hands kept clear of linkages during operation.
  • Hydraulic setup safe: syringes secured, tubing tight, no leaks; spill protocol and cleanup ready.

Function Checks (Before Data)

  • De-air the system: cycle each syringe until motion is smooth and consistent.
  • Confirm full range of motion: boom, arm and bucket (or the chosen joints) move without binding.
  • Check for flex: base stable and joints/pivots tight enough to hold position under load.
  • Identify one safe “test load” method (e.g., lifting a known mass or pulling a spring scale).

Measurement & Data

  • Record syringe sizes (diameter or volume) and calculate piston areas (show units).
  • Record at least 3 trials: input effort vs output load (or force) for one joint.
  • Measure travel: input stroke vs output movement to calculate VR; compute MA and efficiency.
  • Compare predicted vs measured results (table + short explanation for differences).

Evidence Capture

  • Photos: design sketch, build stages, plumbing/de-airing, final assembly, testing setup.
  • Short video (optional): one full operating cycle showing smooth motion and control.
  • Reflection drafted: what worked, what failed first (leaks/air/friction/flex), and one improvement.
  • Folio organised: labelled headings, tables, units, and clear screenshots/diagrams.
