Learning Focus

By the end of this toolkit, you will be able to:

  • Construct the digger frame: Build the base, boom, dipper, and bucket sections accurately according to your Toolkit 2 design.
  • Set up hydraulic system: Connect syringes and tubing, ensuring secure connections and proper routing.
  • Remove air from system: Bleed air bubbles to ensure responsive, efficient operation.
  • Assemble complete digger: Mount cylinders, connect arm sections, and verify all pivots move smoothly.
  • Prepare for testing: Complete pre-test inspection and safety checks before moving to Toolkit 4.

Timing: Build your digger in Weeks 2-4 based on your Toolkit 2 design. Complete construction before starting testing in Toolkit 4. Quality construction here ensures reliable test results.

Timing: Build your digger in Weeks 2-4 based on your Toolkit 2 design. Follow this protocol systematically to ensure quality construction.

1) Frame Assembly

  • Cutting: Cut timber/plywood to planned dimensions from your Toolkit 2 design. Use accurate measurement - mark from a datum edge to reduce error build-up.
  • Drilling: Drill pivot holes accurately - misalignment causes binding and reduces efficiency. Use a drill press or guide to ensure holes are perpendicular and properly aligned.
  • Assembly: Use clamps and squares to ensure joints are square and members align properly. Check that pivot points are correctly positioned according to your design.
  • Quality checks: Measure base dimensions, check that arm sections are the correct lengths, verify pivot holes align properly. Make adjustments before final assembly.

2) Hydraulic System Setup

  • Syringe selection: Use the syringes you selected in Toolkit 2 (typically smaller for input, larger for output). Check that syringes move smoothly - replace any that are stiff or damaged.
  • Tubing connection: Connect tubing securely using cable ties or clamps to prevent leaks. Plan routing to minimise bends and avoid kinks. Keep tubing lengths reasonable - longer tubing increases friction.
  • Filling syringes: Fill syringes completely with water, avoiding air bubbles. Work slowly and carefully to prevent introducing air. Use clean water to avoid contamination.
  • System layout: Arrange syringes according to your Toolkit 2 design. Ensure input syringes are accessible for operation, and output syringes are positioned to connect to arm sections.

3) Bleeding Air from System

Critical step: Air bubbles compress under pressure, creating a "spongy" feel and reducing force transfer. Proper de-airing is essential for good performance.

  • Initial bleeding: Push fluid through the entire system before connecting to the digger. Watch for bubbles and continue until the system feels firm and responsive.
  • Cycling: Operate each cylinder through its full range several times, watching for bubbles. Continue until no bubbles appear and the system feels solid.
  • Final check: Test each cylinder independently - it should move smoothly without sponginess. If air remains, repeat the bleeding process.
  • Documentation: Note any persistent air issues in your build log - these may affect test results and should be mentioned in your evaluation.

4) Mounting Cylinders

  • Bracket/clamp design: Attach syringes to the frame using brackets or clamps according to your design. Ensure they can move freely without binding.
  • Independent testing: Test each cylinder independently before connecting to the arm sections. Verify smooth operation and full range of motion.
  • Connection points: Connect output syringes to arm sections at the planned attachment points. Ensure connections are secure but allow free movement.
  • Alignment check: Verify that cylinders align properly with arm sections - misalignment causes binding and reduces efficiency.

5) Final Assembly

  • Boom to base: Connect boom to base at the main pivot. Check that it moves smoothly through its full range.
  • Dipper to boom: Connect dipper to boom at the planned pivot point. Verify smooth movement and proper alignment.
  • Bucket to dipper: Connect bucket to dipper. Check that bucket can rotate through its full range.
  • Final checks: Check all pivots move smoothly without binding. Verify that hydraulic circuits operate independently. Test full range of motion for each section.
  • Build variations: Record any changes from your original Toolkit 2 design with photos and notes - these affect performance and should be mentioned in your evaluation.

6) Pre-Test Inspection

  • Check for obvious weaknesses: loose joints, misaligned pivots, kinked tubing, or air bubbles in the system.
  • Confirm load application point (typically bucket centre) and how test weights will be added.
  • Verify safety controls: digger is clamped/secured, test area is clear, electrical equipment is away from water, and PPE is ready.
  • Record any build flaws or modifications in your folio so you can refer to them when explaining test results in Toolkit 4.

Build Quality Considerations

Accuracy Matters

Small errors in construction can significantly affect performance:

  • Pivot alignment: Misaligned pivots cause binding and reduce efficiency. Use jigs or guides to ensure accuracy.
  • Lever lengths: Incorrect lever lengths change mechanical advantage from your design predictions.
  • Joint quality: Loose joints reduce stiffness and can cause unexpected movement or failure.

Hydraulic System Quality

The hydraulic system is critical to performance:

  • Leak prevention: Secure connections prevent leaks that reduce pressure and cause "drift".
  • Air removal: Complete de-airing ensures responsive, efficient operation.
  • Tubing condition: Avoid kinks and sharp bends that increase friction and reduce flow.
  • Syringe condition: Smooth-moving syringes reduce friction and improve efficiency.

3) Build Quality Assessment

  • Assess your construction against your Toolkit 2 design.
  • Identify any construction issues that might affect performance.
  • Document these in your folio for reference during testing and evaluation.

4) Google Classroom Check-in
