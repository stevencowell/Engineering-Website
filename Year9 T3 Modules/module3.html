Mechanisms

Learning Focus

  • Design compound mechanisms that dispense lollies reliably and safely.
  • Calculate overall gear ratios and link them to torque, speed and dispensing amounts.
  • Plan for user experience, tolerance control and risk management in small machines.

Key Notes

From simple to compound mechanisms

Compound gear trains, cams, ratchets and springs combine simple machines to perform controlled sequences. The design challenge is to store lollies and dispense them reliably, safely and controllably.

Gear trains in more detail

Multi-stage gear trains multiply ratios; the overall ratio is the product of each stage. Calculate tooth-count ratios to set how many handle turns dispense one compartment, balancing speed and torque.

Cams, followers and motion profiles

Cams convert rotary motion into custom follower movement. Use displacement diagrams to plan when the gate opens; a radial or snail cam can keep the gate closed until alignment, then lift to release lollies.

Reliability, jamming and tolerance

Clearances prevent jams while maintaining control. Select materials to reduce friction and wear, and test prototypes with different lollies. Track jam frequency, wear points and how tolerances affect performance.

Aesthetics, user interaction and ethics

Consider ergonomic handles, viewing windows and appealing graphics. Address hygiene, sustainability of materials and ethical questions about encouraging sugar consumption.

Risk management in dispenser construction

Hazards include cutting and drilling materials, pinch points in gears and choking risks from small parts. Use enclosed gearboxes, clamps and safe storage to control risks.

Activities & Evidence

  • Sketch the gear train, cam or ratchet layout and calculate the overall ratio for one dispense cycle.
  • Prototype the dispensing gate, record jam frequency with different lollies and adjust tolerances.
  • Measure handle torque and output speed to confirm design targets; document with photos.
  • Prepare a user feedback survey on ergonomics, hygiene and clarity of operation.

Q&A

How is overall gear ratio calculated?
Multiply the ratio of each gear pair. For example, a 20:40 pair (1:2) driving a 15:45 pair (1:3) gives an overall 1:6 reduction.
What causes gear trains to jam?
Tight tolerances, misalignment, debris or sticky lollies can jam gears. Providing clearance, smooth surfaces and guards reduces jamming.
How do cams control dispensing?
A cam profile lifts a follower at a set point in the rotation, opening the gate only when compartments align. The dwell keeps the gate closed for the rest of the cycle.
Why consider ethics in a lolly dispenser?
Design choices affect hygiene, waste and user health. Selecting sustainable materials and discussing sugar consumption aligns with EGT5-ENV-01 and responsible engineering.

Resources

  • Gear kits, cams, ratchets, springs, acrylic or timber sheets, adhesives and fasteners.
  • Files, drills and sanding tools for fitting parts; 3D printing or laser cutting for precision components.
  • Templates for user surveys, jam log sheets and risk assessments for cutting and assembly tasks.
