CAD & Folios

Learning Focus

  • Communicate engineering work using briefs, drawings, costing and structured folios.
  • Produce orthographic and pictorial drawings in Onshape for the water tower, digger and dispenser.
  • Create costing spreadsheets and simple schedules that track materials, time and contingencies.

Key Notes

Why engineering communication matters

Engineering projects rely on clear briefs, planning, drawings, costings and reports. Stage 5 outcomes EGT5-COM-01 and EGT5-ENV-01 emphasise accurate communication and sustainability in every project.

Interpreting and producing engineering drawings

Read and create orthographic and pictorial drawings, apply dimensioning and tolerance standards, and use Onshape to model and document the water tower, hydraulic digger and lolly dispenser. Clarity and accuracy are essential.

Costing and simple project management

Use spreadsheets to calculate quantities, unit costs, totals and contingencies. Build formulas, compare design options by cost and outline milestones or simple schedules to plan builds.

Folios and engineering reports

Structure folios with the design brief, constraints, research, concept sketches, selection matrices, working drawings/CAD prints, production logs, test data and evaluation. Reflect on learning and proposed improvements.

Sustainability and lifecycle thinking

Consider material choices, recycling and reuse of components, energy consumption and lifecycle impacts. Propose sustainable alternatives and justify them in your folio.

Careers and pathways

Research mechanical, civil, mechatronic and industrial engineering careers. Connect classroom projects to real-world applications and outline training pathways such as TAFE, apprenticeships and university.

Activities & Evidence

  • Produce orthographic and pictorial drawings for key parts of each Term 3 project using Onshape.
  • Build a costing sheet that totals quantities, unit prices, contingencies and compares design options.
  • Draft a folio outline with sections for brief, research, concepts, CAD outputs, production log and testing.
  • Summarise sustainability considerations and career research within the folio reflection.

Q&A

Why use both orthographic and pictorial drawings?
Orthographic drawings give accurate dimensions for manufacturing, while pictorials communicate form and assembly. Together they improve clarity for builders and reviewers.
What should a costing spreadsheet include?
List items, quantities, unit prices, totals and contingencies. Use formulas to update totals automatically and compare alternate designs or suppliers.
How can sustainability be shown in the folio?
Note recycled or low-impact materials, minimise waste in cutting plans, record energy use for manufacturing and discuss how the design could be reused or adapted.
Why is reflective writing important?
Reflection captures what worked, what failed and how to improve. It demonstrates learning progress and links decisions to outcomes, supporting EGT5-COM-01.
